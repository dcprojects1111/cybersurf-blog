  # Fake ChatGPT Download Sites Are Targeting Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

If you've searched online for ChatGPT recently, you might have stumbled across what looks like a legitimate download page. But cyber criminals are counting on exactly that — and they're now infecting both Windows and Mac users with malware through cleverly disguised fake ChatGPT websites.

For Sunshine Coast small business owners running everything from their laptop or phone, this is exactly the kind of threat that can bring your operations to a grinding halt.

## What's Happening?

Scammers have created fake websites that look almost identical to the real ChatGPT site. When you search for "ChatGPT download" or similar terms, these fraudulent pages can appear in search results, sometimes even at the top.

Here's what makes this threat particularly dangerous:

- The fake sites are designed to look professional and legitimate
- They offer separate "downloads" for both Windows and Mac users
- Each version contains malware specifically tailored to that operating system
- Once installed, the malware can steal passwords, banking details, and access to your business accounts

The cyber criminals behind these sites know that small business owners are increasingly interested in using AI tools like ChatGPT to write emails, create social media posts, or handle customer enquiries. They're exploiting that interest.

## Why Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Are at Risk

You might think, "I'm just a small operation — why would anyone target me?" But that's exactly what makes you vulnerable.

Imagine you're a landscaper on the Sunshine Coast who's heard ChatGPT can help you write better quotes and customer emails. You do a quick Google search on your work laptop between jobs, find what looks like the official download page, and click install. Within minutes, malware is running on your device.

Now consider what's on that laptop:

- Your Xero or MYOB login details
- Saved passwords for your business banking
- Customer contact information and payment details
- Access to your email account and supplier accounts

A single fake download could give criminals access to your entire business operation. They could lock you out of your accounting software, redirect payments meant for suppliers into their own accounts, or steal your customers' personal information — which you're legally responsible for protecting.

## It's Not Just Windows Users Anymore

For years, Mac users felt relatively safe from malware. Those days are over.

Cyber criminals now create separate malware versions for Mac and Windows, meaning no one is protected just by their choice of computer. Whether you're managing your café's books on a MacBook or running your electrical business from a Windows laptop, these fake ChatGPT sites have you covered.

## How to Protect Your Business

The good news is that staying safe doesn't require technical expertise:

**Use the official website only**
- ChatGPT is primarily a web-based tool at chat.openai.com
- You don't need to download software for most uses
- If you're unsure, type the address directly rather than clicking search results

**Double-check before downloading anything**
- Look carefully at the web address (URL) before downloading
- Be suspicious of sites asking you to download ChatGPT as a desktop app
- When in doubt, don't click

**Keep your devices updated**
- Enable automatic updates on your computer and phone
- These updates include security patches that protect against known malware
