  # AI Coding Tools Under Fire: What the 'Ghostcommit' Attack Means for Your Sunshine Coast Business

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, chances are you've started using AI tools to save time. Maybe ChatGPT helps you write social media posts, or you've tried an AI assistant to handle customer enquiries. These tools are brilliant—until they're not.

A new cybersecurity threat called 'Ghostcommit' has just shown how easily AI systems can be tricked into handing over your sensitive information. While this particular attack targeted software developers, it's a wake-up call for every small business owner using AI-powered tools.

## What Is Ghostcommit?

Researchers recently discovered a clever way to fool AI systems. They hid malicious instructions inside ordinary image files—the kind you might attach to an email or upload to a website. When AI tools processed these images, they unknowingly followed hidden commands that told them to steal passwords, access codes, and other confidential information.

The scary part? The AI tools never suspected a thing. They just did what they were told.

## Why This Matters for Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

You might be thinking, "I'm just a tradie with five staff—why would anyone target me?" That's exactly what makes you vulnerable.

Imagine this: You run a landscaping business in Noosa. A potential client emails you photos of their property for a quote. You upload those images to an AI tool that helps you draft proposals and calculate materials. Hidden in one of those images is a prompt injection that tricks the AI into reading your saved passwords or accessing your business banking details.

Or picture this scenario: You own a busy café in Mooloolaba. You use an AI-powered scheduling app to manage your roster. Someone uploads a doctored image to the system—maybe through a job application or supplier catalogue. The AI reads the hidden instructions and quietly sends your employee data, supplier invoices, or customer loyalty information to a stranger.

The common thread? You never see it coming because you trusted the tool to work safely.

## How Prompt Injection Attacks Work

Think of AI tools like incredibly obedient assistants. They follow instructions—sometimes too well. Cybercriminals have worked out how to embed secret instructions inside images, documents, or even website content. When your AI tool processes that file, it reads those hidden commands and acts on them.

In the Ghostcommit demonstration, AI code review tools were completely bypassed. The systems never even looked at the image files. Then, when a coding assistant did process the image, it was tricked into:

- Reading confidential access codes stored in the system
- Converting that sensitive data into a format that looked harmless
- Embedding those secrets directly into code where attackers could grab them

No alarms rang. No security warnings appeared. The AI just quietly handed over the keys.

## Protecting Your Business from AI-Related Threats

**The good news:** You don't need to abandon AI tools. You do need to treat them like any other business system—with proper security measures.

**Here's what smart small business cyber security looks like:**

- Never upload sensitive documents or images to AI tools without understanding where that data goes
- Use separate passwords for different business systems—especially anything connected to banking or customer data
- Regularly check if your business credentials have appeared in data breaches online
- Consider an IT security audit, even if you're a one-person operation

**Warning signs you might already be affected:**

- Unexpected password reset emails you didn't request
- Customers mentioning they received strange communications from "you"
- Unusual activity in your business banking or payment apps
- Staff reporting odd behaviour from business software
