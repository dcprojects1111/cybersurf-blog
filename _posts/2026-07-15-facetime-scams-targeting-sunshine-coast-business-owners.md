  # FaceTime Scams Are Targeting Aussie Business Owners — Here's What You Need to Know

If you run a small business on the Sunshine Coast, your phone isn't just your lifeline to customers—it's also becoming a target for scammers using surprisingly simple tactics to empty bank accounts.

A new wave of cybercriminals is combining old-fashioned manipulation with apps you use every day—like FaceTime—to trick business owners into handing over their banking details. And because most of us are running our businesses from our phones without IT support, we're more vulnerable than we think.

## How the Scam Actually Works

This isn't your typical dodgy text message. These scammers are getting more sophisticated, and they're betting on the fact that you're busy, distracted, and trusting.

Here's what's happening:

**The Setup**
- You receive a FaceTime call (or similar video call) from what appears to be your bank, the ATO, or even a supplier
- The caller looks professional, sounds legitimate, and has just enough of your business details to seem credible
- They create urgency: "suspicious activity on your account," "your ABN verification has expired," or "an overdue invoice needs immediate payment"

**The Hook**
- During the call, they'll guide you to "verify" your identity or "secure" your account
- They might ask you to download an app, click a link they've sent via SMS, or log into your banking while they "help" you
- What you don't realise is they're watching your screen, recording your keystrokes, or installing malicious software

**The Sting**
- Once they have your credentials, they move fast—often draining accounts within hours
- By the time you realise what's happened, the money is gone and the trail is cold

## Why Small Business Owners Are Prime Targets

If you're a tradie, café owner, or accommodation host on the Sunshine Coast, you might think you're too small to be targeted. The opposite is true.

Cybercriminals know that:
- You're managing everything from your phone—banking, bookings, invoicing, payments
- You don't have an IT team to warn you about threats
- You're busy and likely to take a shortcut if someone "official" is offering to help
- Your devices probably aren't up to date with the latest security patches

Imagine you're managing your Airbnb listing between check-ins. You get a FaceTime call from someone claiming to be from Airbnb saying there's a payment issue with your account. They sound professional, they know your property address, and you're in a rush. You follow their instructions… and suddenly both your Airbnb account and linked bank account are compromised.

## The Unpatched Device Problem

Here's something most business owners don't realise: when your phone nags you to install that software update, it's not just adding new emojis. Those updates patch security holes that scammers actively exploit.

An unpatched iPhone or Android device is like leaving your shop unlocked overnight. You might get away with it, but you're making it incredibly easy for someone to walk in and take what they want.

## Protecting Your Business Without an IT Department

You don't need to become a cyber security expert, but you do need to take some basic steps:

**Right Now:**
- Update your phone and all business apps today—yes, even if it takes 20 minutes
- Never accept unexpected video calls from "your bank" or "the ATO"—hang up and call them back on their official number
- Use different passwords for different accounts (at minimum, your banking should have a unique password)

**This Month:**
- Enable two-factor authentication on your banking, booking systems, and invoicing apps
- Review what apps have access to your business data
- Have a conversation with your bank about their fraud protection options

**Ongoing:**
- Be skeptical of urgency—legitimate organisations don't demand immediate action via video call
- Never share your screen or login details during an unsolicited call
- Trust your gut—if something feels off, it probably is
