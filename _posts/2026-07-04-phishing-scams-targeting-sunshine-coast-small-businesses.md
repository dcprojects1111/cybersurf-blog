  # Phishing Scams Are Getting Smarter — Is Your Sunshine Coast Business Ready?

If you run a small business on the Sunshine Coast, you're juggling a hundred things at once. The last thing on your mind is probably cybersecurity. But here's something worth knowing: phishing scams — those dodgy emails and texts trying to trick you into handing over passwords or banking details — are getting much more sophisticated, and they're increasingly targeting sole traders and micro-businesses just like yours.

## What's Changed With Phishing Attacks?

Phishing used to be easy to spot. Bad grammar, weird sender addresses, and clunky messages that screamed "scam" from a mile away. Not anymore.

Today's phishing attacks use artificial intelligence to craft messages that look and sound completely legitimate. They might appear to come from your bank, Australia Post, the ATO, Xero, Square, or even one of your regular suppliers. The language is spot-on, the branding looks real, and the sense of urgency feels genuine.

For small business owners who manage everything from their phone — often while on a job site, between clients, or during a quick coffee break — it's becoming harder than ever to tell the real from the fake.

## Why Small Businesses Are Prime Targets

You might think cybercriminals only go after big corporations, but that's not the case. Small businesses are actually attractive targets because:

- **Less protection**: Unlike large companies, most sole traders and micro-businesses don't have IT security audits, firewalls, or dedicated support
- **Quick access to money**: Small business accounts often have direct links to personal banking and payment systems
- **Busy owners**: When you're rushing between jobs or customers, you're more likely to click first and question later
- **Valuable data**: Customer contact details, booking information, and supplier logins are all worth money to scammers

## A Real-World Scenario: The Landscaper Who Nearly Lost Everything

Imagine you're a landscaper finishing up a job in Buderim. You get an email on your phone that looks like it's from your invoicing software, saying there's a problem with your account and you need to verify your details immediately or your service will be suspended.

You're tired, you've got two more jobs today, and you need to send invoices tonight to keep cash flow moving. So you click the link and enter your username and password.

Within hours, the scammer has access to your account. They can see your customer list, your banking details, and your entire job schedule. They change your payment details so your next customer payments go to their account instead of yours. By the time you realise something's wrong, they've disappeared with thousands of dollars — and you're left explaining to your customers why their payments went astray.

This isn't a far-fetched scenario. It's happening to small business owners across Australia every single week.

## How to Protect Your Business From Phishing

The good news is that awareness is your best defence. Here are practical steps you can take today:

**Before You Click:**
- Pause and think, even when a message seems urgent
- Check the sender's email address carefully — scammers often use addresses that are one letter different from the real thing
- Hover over links (don't click) to see where they actually lead
- If in doubt, contact the company directly using a phone number you find yourself, not one in the message

**Set Up Basic Protection:**
- Enable two-factor authentication on all your business apps and accounts
- Use different passwords for different accounts
- Update your phone and apps regularly — those updates include security patches
- Back up important business data regularly

**Stay Informed:**
- Cyber security on the Sunshine Coast doesn't require a big IT department — it starts with staying alert
- Talk to other business owners about scams they've encountered
- Follow Scamwatch for alerts about new small business cyber security threats
