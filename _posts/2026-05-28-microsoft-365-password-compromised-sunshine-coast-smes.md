  # Sunshine Coast Businesses: Why Your Microsoft 365 Password Might Already Be Compromised

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast — whether you're managing short-stay accommodation, running a salon, or operating a trades business — there's a good chance you're using Microsoft 365 for email, invoicing, or keeping track of your customers. What many local business owners don't realise is that cybercriminals are actively targeting these accounts, and yours might already be on their radar.

## The Reality of Password Breaches for Small Business Owners

Here's how it typically happens: you sign up for a new booking system, supplier portal, or industry website using the same email and password you use for your Microsoft 365 account. That third-party site gets hacked (which happens more often than you'd think), and suddenly your login details are floating around on the dark web. Cybercriminals buy these credentials in bulk and systematically test them against Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and other business platforms.

The scary part? You won't know it's happened until it's too late.

## A Real Threat for Sunshine Coast Businesses

Imagine you're an Airbnb host with three properties in Mooloolaba. You check your emails one morning and find you've been locked out of your Microsoft account. By the time you regain access, a scammer has:

- Sent fake invoices to your upcoming guests asking them to pay a "deposit" to a different bank account
- Changed your listing passwords and contacted guests pretending to be you
- Accessed your supplier contacts and attempted to scam them using your trusted name

This isn't a hypothetical scenario — it's happening to Australian small businesses every week. And because most sole traders and micro-businesses on the Sunshine Coast don't have IT support, they often don't discover the breach until significant damage has been done.

## How Compromised Passwords Lead to Business Disruption

When criminals access your Microsoft 365 account, they can:

- Read all your emails and find sensitive business or customer information
- Send emails pretending to be you to scam your clients or suppliers
- Access your OneDrive files, including quotes, contracts, and financial records
- Lock you out by changing your password and recovery details
- Use your account as a launching pad to target your customers

For a tradie running their business from their phone, losing access to your email can mean missed jobs, delayed quotes, and damage to your professional reputation that takes months to repair.

## Small Business Cyber Security Starts with Knowing Your Risk

The good news is that checking whether your credentials have been compromised is straightforward. Unlike large companies that have dedicated IT teams, small business cyber security doesn't need to be complicated or expensive. What matters most is knowing where you stand.

Many Sunshine Coast SMEs assume that because they're small, they're not a target. The opposite is true. Cybercriminals use automated tools that don't discriminate based on business size — they simply test every compromised password they can find. An SME data breach can be just as devastating to a five-person business as a major hack is to a corporation, often more so because you lack the resources to recover quickly.
