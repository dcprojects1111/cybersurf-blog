If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast—whether you're a tradie, salon owner, or short-stay accommodation host—there's something you need to know. ## What Are Stolen Credentials and Why Should You Care?

Your login credentials are simply your usernames and passwords for the various accounts you use to run your business. Think about how many you have: your MYOB or Xero account, your booking system, your business email, your Facebook and Instagram business pages, maybe your supplier portals or trade accounts.

When cybercriminals steal these credentials—often through data breaches at large companies—they don't always use them immediately. Instead, they sell them in bulk on underground marketplaces. Other criminals then buy these credentials and systematically try them across different platforms, knowing that most people reuse the same password everywhere.

## How This Could Impact Your Sunshine Coast Business

Let me paint you a picture. Imagine you're a plumber on the Sunshine Coast with 3 staff. You use the same password for your email, your accounting software, and your job management app because it's easier to remember. 

One day, a company you created an account with years ago—maybe a supplier or an online tool you tried once—gets breached. Your email and password from that old account are now for sale. A cybercriminal buys a list of 10,000 credentials for $50 and starts testing them.

Within hours, they've accessed your:

- **Business email** – reading confidential client communications and sending invoicing scams to your customers
- **Accounting software** – viewing your financial data and potentially locking you out
- **Job scheduling system** – disrupting your entire week's work

The cost to your business isn't just the immediate chaos. It's the loss of customer trust, the hours spent recovering access, potential regulatory issues with client data, and lost income while you sort it all out.

## The SME Data Breach Reality on the Sunshine Coast

Small business cyber security often takes a back seat when you're juggling a dozen other priorities. But here's the reality: cybercriminals specifically target small and medium enterprises (SMEs) because they know you likely don't have IT support or sophisticated security measures.

An SME data breach doesn't just happen to "other businesses." Sunshine Coast businesses—from Noosa to Caloundra—are being targeted right now because criminals know that:

- Most sole traders and micro-businesses use simple, reused passwords
- They run everything from their phone with minimal security
- They're unlikely to have two-factor authentication enabled
- They won't notice a breach until significant damage is done
