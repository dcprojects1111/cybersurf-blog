## How Criminals Are Breaking Into Your Accounts

Cybercriminals use several tactics to gain access to your business accounts:

**Credential Stuffing**
- Hackers obtain usernames and passwords from previous data breaches
- They automatically test these credentials across hundreds of websites
- If you've reused the same password across multiple accounts, they're in

**Phishing Attacks**
- You receive what looks like a legitimate email from your bank, accounting software, or booking platform
- The email asks you to verify your details or click a link
- Once you enter your information, criminals have everything they need

**Weak Password Exploitation**
- Simple passwords like "password123" or your business name
- Easily guessed security questions
- No two-factor authentication protecting your accounts

## What's at Stake for Your Business

An SME data breach isn't just inconvenient—it can be devastating for a small business:

- **Lost revenue**: You can't access your invoicing or booking systems
- **Customer trust**: Your clients' personal information may be compromised
- **Reputation damage**: Word spreads quickly in close-knit communities like the Sunshine Coast
- **Financial loss**: Fraudulent transactions, recovery costs, and potential legal issues
- **Business downtime**: Days or weeks spent trying to regain control of your accounts

For an Airbnb host, it could mean scammers taking over your listing and pocketing guest payments. For a hairdresser, it might be losing access to months of bookings and customer contact details. For an accountant, it could mean exposing sensitive client financial information.

## Small Business Cyber Security Starts With Awareness

The good news is that protecting yourself doesn't require becoming a tech expert. Basic small business cyber security practices can dramatically reduce your risk:

- Use unique, strong passwords for every business account
- Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible
- Be suspicious of unexpected emails asking for login details
- Regularly check which devices are logged into your accounts
- Keep your phone and apps updated
