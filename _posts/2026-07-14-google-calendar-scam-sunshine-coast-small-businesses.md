  # New Google Calendar Scam Targets Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Through Fake Meeting Invites

If you run your business from your phone—whether you're a landscaper managing site visits, an accountant juggling client meetings, or a café owner coordinating supplier deliveries—chances are Google Calendar is one of your most-used apps. Unfortunately, cybercriminals know this too, and they've found a clever way to exploit it.

A sophisticated new scam is hitting Australian small businesses through fake Google Calendar invitations. These aren't your typical spam emails that get caught by filters—they're calendar invites that appear directly in your schedule, looking completely legitimate until it's too late.

## How the Google Calendar Scam Works

The scam is deceptively simple but alarmingly effective. Cybercriminals send calendar invitations that appear to come from genuine businesses or contacts. These invites automatically populate your calendar—often without you even clicking accept—thanks to Google's default settings.

Here's what makes this particularly dangerous for Sunshine Coast small business owners:

**The invites look professional**
- They use real company names and logos
- They reference plausible meeting topics
- They include links that appear to be Zoom meetings or document shares

**They bypass traditional security**
- No suspicious email to flag
- They come through Google's legitimate calendar system
- Your phone simply notifies you of a new appointment

**The damage happens when you click**
- Links lead to fake login pages designed to steal your passwords
- Some direct you to malicious websites that install spyware
- Others harvest your personal and business information

## Why This Matters for Your Business

Imagine you're a sparkie between jobs, and a calendar invite pops up claiming to be from a major Sunshine Coast builder about a new commercial project. You click the link to "confirm your availability," enter your Google login details on what looks like a standard sign-in page, and suddenly scammers have access to everything connected to that account—your emails, contacts, documents, and financial records.

This type of SME data breach doesn't just compromise your calendar. Once criminals have your Google credentials, they can:

- Lock you out of your entire Google Workspace
- Access your business banking if you use autofill
- Impersonate you to scam your clients and suppliers
- Steal sensitive customer data, creating legal problems for your business

For sole traders and micro-businesses without IT support, this kind of cyber security breach can be devastating. You're not just dealing with lost access—you're dealing with potential identity theft, financial loss, and damaged reputation.

## Protecting Your Business From Calendar Scams

The good news is that small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated. Here are practical steps you can take today:

**Adjust your Google Calendar settings**
- Turn off automatic event adding from Gmail
- Require manual acceptance for all calendar invites
- Review who can see your calendar and send you invites

**Stay alert to red flags**
- Unexpected meetings from people you don't know
- Invites with unusual links, especially shortened URLs
- Meeting requests about jobs or opportunities you haven't discussed

**Verify before you click**
- If an invite looks suspicious, contact the sender directly using details you already have
- Never click links in calendar invites from unknown sources
- Be especially cautious with invites requesting urgent action

**Strengthen your account security**
- Enable two-factor authentication on all business accounts
- Use unique passwords for different services
- Consider an IT security audit to identify vulnerabilities
