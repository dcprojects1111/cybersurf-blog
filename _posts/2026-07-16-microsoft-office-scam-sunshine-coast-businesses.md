  # New Microsoft Office Scam Targeting Australian Small Businesses

## You Don't Need to Click a Link to Get Hacked Anymore

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast and rely on Microsoft 365 for your emails, invoicing, or bookkeeping, you need to know about a sophisticated new scam that's catching out business owners across Australia.

Unlike the clumsy phishing emails of the past, this attack doesn't need you to click anything. It works by flooding your Microsoft account with password reset notifications until you're so overwhelmed that you accidentally approve one — and just like that, the scammers are in.

## How the "MFA Fatigue" Attack Works

Cybercriminals have already stolen your email and password — usually from an old data breach you didn't even know about. They try to log into your Microsoft account, which triggers a notification on your phone asking you to approve the sign-in.

Here's where it gets nasty:

- They send dozens, sometimes hundreds of these approval requests
- Your phone buzzes non-stop, day and night
- You're trying to finish a job, deal with customers, or just get some sleep
- Eventually, you tap "Approve" just to make it stop
- The scammers are now inside your account

Security experts call this "MFA fatigue" — and it's becoming one of the most effective ways to bypass two-factor authentication, even when you've done the right thing and turned it on.

## What Happens When They Get In

Imagine you're an electrician finishing up a big commercial job. You've been getting these annoying Microsoft notifications all afternoon. Frustrated, you approve one without thinking. By the time you realise what's happened:

- Your invoicing software has been locked
- Emails to clients are bouncing
- Your payment details in Xero or MYOB have been changed
- Thousands of dollars are being redirected to an overseas account

This isn't a hypothetical scenario. It's happening to tradies, retail shop owners, and service businesses right here in Queensland. The financial and reputational damage can be devastating, especially when you're operating on tight margins.

## How to Protect Yourself From MFA Fatigue Attacks

**Never approve a login notification you didn't initiate.** If your phone starts buzzing with approval requests and you're not trying to log in, don't tap anything. Decline them all.

**Check if your credentials are already compromised.** Many SME data breaches happen because your login details were exposed years ago and you never knew about it.

**Use number matching.** Newer Microsoft accounts offer "number matching" where you have to enter a specific number shown on the login screen. This stops automated attacks cold.

**Use an authenticator app, not SMS.** Text message codes can be intercepted. Apps like Microsoft Authenticator are more secure.

**Change your password if you're being bombarded.** If the notifications won't stop, your current password is definitely compromised.
