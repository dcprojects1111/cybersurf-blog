# Cyber Criminals Are Exploiting QR Codes: What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you've ever scanned a QR code to view a menu at a café or receive payment for a job, you need to read this. Cyber criminals have found a clever new way to trick busy business owners, and it's happening right now across Australia.

## What's Happening with QR Code Scams?

QR codes have become part of everyday business life on the Sunshine Coast. We use them for COVID check-ins, contactless payments, menu displays, and even marketing materials. But security experts are now warning that scammers are taking advantage of how much we trust these little black-and-white squares.

The scam works like this: criminals create fake QR codes that look legitimate but actually direct you to fraudulent websites. These sites might look identical to your banking app, your business software login page, or even a payment portal you use every day.

## Why Small Business Owners Are Prime Targets

Here's the thing most sole traders and micro-businesses don't realise: you're actually more attractive to cyber criminals than big companies. Why? Because you typically:

- Run your business from your phone without dedicated IT security
- Don't have time to verify every link or code you scan
- Use the same password across multiple business apps
- Trust that official-looking communications are genuine

Let me paint you a picture. Imagine you're a plumber finishing up a job in Noosa. The client says they'll pay immediately and shows you a QR code to scan for the payment details. You scan it, enter your banking app credentials, and... nothing happens. You assume it's a glitch, try another payment method, and move on.

What you don't know is that scammer now has your banking login details. By the time you're at your next job, they could be draining your business account.

## Real Threats for Sunshine Coast Businesses

This isn't just theoretical. These QR code scams are being used to:

- Steal business banking credentials
- Take over social media and booking system accounts (imagine losing access to your salon's appointment calendar right before a busy weekend)
- Install malware that captures every password you type
- Redirect customer payments to criminal accounts

For accommodation hosts, the risk is particularly high. Scammers are creating fake QR codes that appear to be from Airbnb or Stayz, asking you to "verify your account" or "update payment details." One scan could give criminals access to your entire listing, guest information, and payment history.

## How to Protect Your Business

The good news? You don't need to be a tech expert to stay safe. Here are practical steps you can take today:

**Before Scanning Any QR Code:**

- Check if the code has been tampered with or stuck over another code
- If receiving payment, ask for alternative verification
- Never scan codes from unsolicited emails or text messages
- Look carefully at the URL before entering any login details

**General Cyber Security for Small Business:**

- Enable two-factor authentication on every business app you use
- Use different passwords for different accounts (yes, even if it's harder to remember)
- Be suspicious of any urgent requests to scan codes or click links
- Regularly check your business accounts for unusual activity
