  # Cybercriminals Are Targeting QuickBooks, Xero and MYOB Users – Is Your Business at Risk?

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, there's a good chance you're using cloud accounting software like QuickBooks, Xero, or MYOB to manage your invoices, track expenses, and stay on top of the books. These platforms have made life so much easier for sole traders and micro-businesses – but they've also become a major target for cybercriminals.

Recent reports show that hackers are increasingly targeting popular accounting platforms, and the consequences for small business owners can be devastating. Let's talk about what's happening, why it matters to you, and what you can do to protect yourself.

## Why Are Hackers Going After Accounting Software?

It's simple: your accounting software contains everything a criminal needs. Bank account details, customer information, supplier contacts, transaction history, tax file numbers – it's all there in one convenient place.

For a cybercriminal, gaining access to your QuickBooks or Xero account is like finding the keys to your entire business. They can:

- Redirect payments to their own accounts
- Steal customer data for identity fraud
- Lock you out and demand a ransom
- Use your business details to commit further fraud
- Access connected bank accounts

And here's the thing – many small business owners on the Sunshine Coast are running their entire operation from their phone, often with minimal password protection and no backup plan if something goes wrong.

## How a Café Owner Lost $15,000 in One Afternoon

Imagine this: you're a café owner in Noosa, rushing between the coffee machine and the till during the morning rush. You get an email that looks like it's from Xero, saying there's a problem with your account. You click the link, enter your password, and think nothing of it.

By that afternoon, a hacker has logged into your accounting software, changed your banking details, and redirected three supplier payments – totaling $15,000 – into their own account. You don't notice until your coffee supplier calls asking why their invoice is overdue.

This isn't a made-up scenario. SME data breaches like this are happening regularly across Australia, and small businesses are often the easiest targets because they typically don't have IT security audits or proper cyber security measures in place.

## Warning Signs Your Accounting Software May Be Compromised

Keep an eye out for these red flags:

- Unexpected password reset emails you didn't request
- Invoices or transactions you don't recognize
- Being suddenly locked out of your account
- Customers or suppliers saying they received unusual payment requests from you
- Email notifications about changed banking details or contact information

## Simple Steps to Protect Your Business

The good news? You don't need to be a tech expert to protect yourself. Here are practical steps any small business owner can take:

**Strengthen your passwords**
- Use a unique, complex password for your accounting software
- Never reuse passwords across different platforms
- Consider using a password manager app

**Enable two-factor authentication**
- This adds an extra security layer beyond just your password
- Most accounting platforms offer this – turn it on today

**Be suspicious of emails**
- Always verify requests by logging in directly to the platform
- Never click links in emails asking you to "verify your account"
- When in doubt, contact the software provider directly

**Regular check-ins**
- Review your transactions weekly
- Keep an eye on login notifications
- Update your software when prompted
