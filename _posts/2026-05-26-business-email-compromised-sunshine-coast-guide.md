# Has Your Business Email Already Been Compromised? What Sunshine Coast Small Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast—whether you're managing rental properties, running a busy café, or operating a trade business from your ute—your email is probably the backbone of everything you do. Quotes, invoices, customer bookings, supplier orders: it all flows through that one inbox you check on your phone between jobs.

But here's something most sole traders and micro-business owners don't realise: your email account might already be compromised, and you wouldn't even know it.

## The Hidden Threat Sitting in Your Inbox

When we talk about cyber security on the Sunshine Coast, most small business owners picture dramatic movie scenes—hackers in hoodies, flashing screens, total system shutdowns. The reality is far more subtle and, frankly, more dangerous because of it.

Email compromises rarely announce themselves. There's no flashing warning. No locked screen. Instead, cybercriminals gain quiet access to your account and simply watch. They learn your patterns, your suppliers, your customers, and your payment processes. Then, when the timing is perfect, they strike.

## What an Email Breach Actually Looks Like for Small Business

Imagine you're an accountant with a handful of regular clients. You've just completed end-of-financial-year work for a local retailer and emailed through your invoice for $4,500. Your client pays promptly—or so they think. 

Three weeks later, they call asking why you've sent a follow-up reminder when they already paid. Except they didn't pay you. They paid a scammer who'd been quietly monitoring your email, intercepted your legitimate invoice, and sent a near-identical version with different bank details.

This isn't a hypothetical scenario. It's happening right now to small businesses across Australia, and Sunshine Coast SMEs are not immune.

## Common Ways Business Emails Get Compromised

Your email security might already be breached if:

- You've used the same password for multiple accounts (your business email, your supplier portals, that industry forum you signed up for)
- You've ever accessed your email on public Wi-Fi without protection
- You clicked a link in what seemed like a legitimate email from Australia Post, MyGov, or your bank
- Your password hasn't been changed in over a year
- You don't have two-factor authentication enabled

The concerning part? A recent SME data breach study found that compromised credentials are often sold on the dark web months before they're actually used. Your information could be out there right now, just waiting.

## Why Small Business Cyber Security Matters More Than Ever

If you're thinking "I'm just a one-person gardening business, why would anyone target me?"—you're asking the wrong question. 

Cybercriminals aren't necessarily targeting you specifically. They're targeting everyone, using automated tools that scan millions of email accounts looking for easy access. Your size doesn't protect you; it actually makes you more vulnerable because you likely don't have the IT security measures that larger businesses have in place.

And the consequences hit harder when you're small. A compromised Airbnb host account could mean fake bookings, stolen guest payments, and a destroyed reputation that takes years to rebuild. A hacked tradie email could mean lost invoices worth thousands and damaged client relationships.
