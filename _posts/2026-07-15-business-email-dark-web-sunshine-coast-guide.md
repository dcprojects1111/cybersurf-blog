  # Your Business Email Could Be Selling on the Dark Web Right Now — Here's What Sunshine Coast Tradies and Small Business Owners Need to Know

You're juggling quotes, managing staff rosters, chasing invoices, and keeping customers happy — all from your phone. The last thing on your mind is cyber security. ## What's Actually Happening?

When large companies get hacked (think LinkedIn, Adobe, or even Australian retailers), millions of email addresses and passwords get stolen. These lists get sold and shared on underground forums. Criminals then use automated tools to try those same email-and-password combinations on other services — your banking app, your Xero account, your Square payment system, your booking platform.

This is called "credential stuffing," and it works because most of us reuse passwords. You probably created your MYOB login with the same password you've been using since 2015. Maybe it's got your kid's name and a couple of numbers. It feels secure enough — until it isn't.

These aren't scare stories. They're real scenarios playing out across Australian small businesses every week. An SME data breach doesn't always make the news, but it can shut down your cash flow and damage trust in ways that take months to repair.

## What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Can Do Right Now

The good news? You don't need to become a tech expert or hire an IT team. Small business cyber security starts with a few simple, practical steps:

### Protect Your Accounts

- **Use unique passwords for every important account** — especially banking, accounting software, and booking platforms
- **Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)** wherever it's available — it adds a second lock even if your password leaks
- **Use a password manager** like Bitwarden or 1Password to keep track without writing them down

### Stay Alert to the Basics

- **Don't click links in unexpected emails** — even if they look like they're from Australia Post or the ATO
- **Check your account activity regularly** — look for logins from strange locations or devices you don't recognise
- **Update your phone and apps** — those annoying updates often fix security holes

### Know Your Risk

Most Sunshine Coast sole traders and micro-business owners have never done an IT security audit, and that's completely normal. But you wouldn't skip getting your car serviced, and the same logic applies here.
