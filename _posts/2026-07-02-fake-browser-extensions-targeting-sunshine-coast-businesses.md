  # Fake Browser Extensions Are Targeting Small Businesses — Here's What Sunshine Coast Owners Need to Know

If you're running your business from your phone or laptop — whether you're managing bookings for your accommodation, sending quotes as a tradie, or keeping client records for your salon — there's a new threat you need to know about. It's not flashy, it's not obvious, and that's exactly what makes it dangerous.

A fake browser extension recently made headlines after secretly spying on thousands of users' searches. The dodgy add-on mimicked a legitimate AI search tool called Perplexity, and anyone who installed it thinking they were getting a helpful productivity tool actually gave criminals a window into everything they searched for online.

For Sunshine Coast small business owners with no IT support, this is a wake-up call worth paying attention to.

## What Actually Happened?

Cybercriminals created a fake Chrome extension that looked legitimate. It used the name and branding of a real company, appeared in search results when people looked for the tool, and once installed, began quietly monitoring every search the user made.

Think about what you search for in a typical work day:

- Client names and contact details
- Your banking portal
- Xero, MYOB, or other accounting software
- Booking systems and business apps
- Supplier websites where you've saved payment details

Every single search could have been recorded. Every password reset link you clicked. Every sensitive business query you made.

## Why This Matters for Your Sunshine Coast Business

Let's make this personal. Imagine you're an Airbnb host managing three properties around Noosa or Mooloolaba. You've just installed what you thought was a helpful search tool to quickly find answers about tourism regulations or guest questions.

Within days, someone has access to:

- Your Airbnb login details (captured when you searched for the login page)
- Your guest communication patterns
- Your banking searches
- Your pricing strategy research

Before you know it, your listing could be hijacked, your calendar manipulated, or your payout details changed — all because a fake extension was watching everything you did online.

This isn't just about big corporations experiencing an SME data breach. This is about sole traders and micro-businesses becoming targets because criminals know you don't have IT teams protecting you.

## How These Fake Extensions Spread

Browser extensions are small add-ons that promise to make your work easier. Some are legitimate and genuinely helpful. Others are traps.

The fake extensions work because they:

- Use names and logos of real, trusted tools
- Appear in search results when you're looking for the genuine product
- Ask for permissions that seem reasonable
- Work quietly in the background without obvious signs

Most Sunshine Coast small business owners install them without a second thought, especially when you're busy running between jobs or managing multiple clients.

## Protecting Your Business from Extension-Based Threats

Small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated, but it does require awareness. Here's what you can do right now:

**Audit your current extensions:**

- Open Chrome, Edge, or whatever browser you use
- Check what extensions are installed
- Remove anything you don't actively use or don't remember installing

**Before installing new extensions:**

- Only install from official company websites, not search results
- Check the developer name carefully
- Read the permissions it's asking for — if a search tool wants access to your banking sites, that's a red flag
- Look at the number of users and reviews (though these can be faked)

**For day-to-day protection:**

- Don't save passwords in your browser
- Use a dedicated password manager instead
- Enable two-factor authentication on critical business accounts
- Regularly review what's installed on your devices
