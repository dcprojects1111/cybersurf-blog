  # Cryptojacking Scam Hitting Sunshine Coast Businesses Through Google Searches

If you've recently Googled how to download popular software for your business—anything from PDF readers to design tools—you might have unknowingly invited a serious threat onto your laptop or phone.

Cybercriminals are running a sophisticated campaign that's hijacking Google search results and even manipulating AI chatbots to trick business owners into downloading infected software. Once installed, this malicious software secretly uses your computer's power to mine cryptocurrency for criminals, slowing down your devices and potentially opening the door to far worse threats.

For Sunshine Coast small business owners who rely on their devices every single day, this is more than just a tech problem—it's a business continuity issue.

## What Is Cryptojacking and Why Should You Care?

Cryptojacking is when cybercriminals secretly install software on your computer or phone that uses your device's processing power to "mine" cryptocurrency for them. You won't see a ransom note or locked files. Instead, your computer just gets slower, your electricity bill creeps up, and your device's lifespan shortens—all while criminals profit.

But here's the real worry: if malware can get onto your system once, it can happen again. And next time, it might not just be cryptojacking. It could be ransomware that locks you out of your booking system, accounting software, or customer database.

## How the Scam Works: SEO Poisoning Explained

This particular threat uses something called "SEO poisoning." Here's how it works:

**The scam in four steps:**

- Criminals create fake websites that look legitimate, offering popular software downloads
- They use dodgy SEO tactics to push these sites to the top of Google search results
- They've even manipulated AI chatbot responses to recommend these infected downloads
- When you click and install what looks like legitimate software, you're actually installing malware

Imagine you're a builder on the Sunshine Coast looking to download a quoting app you heard about at a job site. You Google it, click the first result, download what looks like the right program—and you've just compromised your business device.

## Who's Really at Risk?

This campaign specifically targets high-performance computers, but don't think your basic laptop is safe. The criminals behind these operations aren't picky. Any Sunshine Coast SME could be affected:

- **Accountants** downloading tax software from a poisoned search result
- **Café owners** looking for point-of-sale system updates
- **Electricians or plumbers** grabbing job management apps between sites
- **Retail shops** installing inventory software

The common thread? Business owners searching Google for software they need, trusting the top results, and installing without a second thought.

## The Real Cost to Your Small Business

Beyond slower devices, cryptojacking malware creates real business risks:

- **Reduced productivity** when computers lag or crash
- **Higher operating costs** from increased power consumption
- **Potential data breaches** if the malware opens backdoors for other attacks
- **Device damage** from overheating components running at full capacity

For a sole trader or micro-business on the Sunshine Coast with no IT support, recovering from even a minor cyber security incident can mean days of lost income.

## How to Protect Your Sunshine Coast Business

**Simple steps you can take today:**

- Only download software from official websites—never from search results without verifying the URL
- Be skeptical of AI chatbot software recommendations
- Keep your devices and software updated with security patches
- Watch for unusual device behavior: overheating, fan running constantly, or sudden slowdowns
