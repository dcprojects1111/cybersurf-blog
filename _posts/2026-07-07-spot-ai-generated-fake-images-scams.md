  # How Sunshine Coast Business Owners Can Spot AI-Generated Fake Images Used in Scams

If you've been running your Sunshine Coast business for any length of time, you've probably noticed something: scammers are getting better. Much better. And one of their newest tricks is using artificial intelligence to create incredibly realistic fake images that make their cons more convincing.

Whether you're a tradie responding to what looks like a legitimate supplier email, an Airbnb host vetting a booking request, or a café owner checking out a new vendor, these AI-generated images can fool even the savviest among us. Let's talk about what you need to know.

## Why This Matters for Small Business Owners

You might be thinking, "I'm just a small operation — why would scammers target me?" The truth is, sole traders and micro-businesses are prime targets precisely because you're busy, you're often working from your phone, and you don't have an IT team watching your back.

Consider this scenario: You're a landscaper on the Sunshine Coast, and you receive an email that appears to be from a machinery hire company. There's a professional-looking photo of equipment, an invoice, and payment details. Everything looks legitimate. You pay the invoice, but the equipment never arrives — because the whole thing, including that photo, was fake.

That's the power of AI-generated images in the hands of scammers, and it's becoming a real problem for small business cyber security.

## What Are AI-Generated Images?

AI-generated images are pictures created entirely by computer programs, not cameras. Modern AI can produce photos of people, products, invoices, and even business premises that never existed. These aren't the clunky, obviously fake images of a few years ago — they're often indistinguishable from real photographs at first glance.

Scammers use these images to:

- Create fake supplier or vendor profiles
- Generate false identity documents
- Produce convincing "proof" photos for elaborate cons
- Build entirely fictional business fronts
- Fabricate evidence in payment scams

## How to Spot an AI-Generated Image

Here's the good news: AI-generated images still have telltale signs if you know what to look for. As a busy business owner, you don't need to become an expert, but knowing these basics can protect you.

### Check the Fine Details

**Hands and fingers**: AI still struggles with hands. Look for extra fingers, missing fingers, or fingers that bend in strange ways.

**Eyes and teeth**: Look closely at reflections in eyes — do they make sense? Are teeth unnaturally uniform or oddly shaped?

**Text in images**: AI-generated images often include gibberish text on signs, labels, or documents that looks like writing but isn't actually readable.

**Backgrounds**: Check where the subject meets the background. Are there odd blurs or discontinuities? Do objects in the background make logical sense?

### Look for Consistency Issues

- Does the lighting make sense across the whole image?
- Are shadows falling in the right directions?
- Do reflections in windows, glasses, or mirrors match what they should show?
- Are patterns (like tiles, brickwork, or fabric) consistent or do they warp strangely?

### Trust Your Instincts

If something feels off about an image — even if you can't pinpoint exactly what — take a moment to investigate further. That's your brain picking up on subtle inconsistencies.

## Practical Steps for Sunshine Coast SMEs

Protecting your business from scams involving AI-generated images doesn't require technical expertise. Here's what you can do:

**Verify independently**: If someone sends you an image as "proof" of something important, verify through another channel. Call the company directly using a number you find yourself, not one provided in the suspicious email.

**Slow down**: Scammers rely on urgency. Take time to examine images in payment requests, new vendor profiles, or unusual communications.

**Educate your team**: If you have staff, make sure they know these scams exist. A quick team chat over coffee about what to watch for can prevent an SME data breach.

**Stay updated**: Cyber security on the Sunshine Coast isn't just for big corporations — it's essential for every business owner.
