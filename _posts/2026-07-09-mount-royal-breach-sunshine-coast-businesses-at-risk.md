  # Mount Royal University Breach Shows Why Even Small Sunshine Coast Businesses Are in the Crosshairs

## Universities Aren't the Only Targets Anymore

You might have seen the news about Mount Royal University in Calgary getting hacked. Cybercriminals broke into their network, stole sensitive data, and then deleted it from their systems. It sounds like something that only happens to big institutions with millions of records, doesn't it?

Here's the uncomfortable truth: the same tactics being used against universities are now being turned on small businesses right here on the Sunshine Coast. And if you're running your business from your phone without IT support, you might be an even easier target.

## What Actually Happened in the Mount Royal Attack

The hackers didn't just peek at files — they stole data and then deliberately deleted it from the university's storage systems. This is a double-hit strategy that's becoming more common:

- First, they steal your sensitive information (client details, financial records, passwords)
- Then they delete or encrypt your files so you can't access them
- Finally, they demand payment either to not release the data publicly or to give it back

This isn't a smash-and-grab anymore. It's calculated, and it's designed to cause maximum disruption.

## Why This Matters to Your Sunshine Coast Business

Let's bring this home. Imagine you're a landscaper with five years of customer records, quotes, and job schedules stored in the cloud. One morning you open your invoicing app and everything's gone. Your client list. Your outstanding invoices. Your supplier contacts. All of it.

Or picture this: you run a short-stay accommodation business on the Coast. Your booking system gets breached. The hackers now have guest names, phone numbers, credit card details, and home addresses. They delete your upcoming reservations and contact your guests pretending to be you.

These aren't scare tactics — these are real scenarios playing out for small businesses across Australia every single week. The Australian Cyber Security Centre reported that small businesses now make up the majority of cybersecurity incidents, not the big companies you read about in the news.

## The "Delete After Stealing" Trend Is Growing

The Mount Royal breach highlights a particularly nasty evolution in cybercrime. It's not enough to just steal data anymore — criminals are wiping systems to:

- Create panic and urgency
- Destroy evidence of how they got in
- Force businesses to pay ransoms faster
- Cover their tracks before security teams respond

For a small business owner without backups or IT security measures in place, this can be absolutely devastating. An SME data breach of this nature can shut down operations for days or even weeks.

## What Small Business Cyber Security Actually Looks Like

You don't need a dedicated IT department to protect yourself, but you do need to take some basic steps:

- **Use proper backups** — and keep them separate from your main systems
- **Enable two-factor authentication** on everything important
- **Use strong, unique passwords** for each business account
- **Keep software updated** on all your devices
- **Know what data you're storing** and where it lives

These fundamentals of cyber security for Sunshine Coast businesses don't require technical expertise, just consistent attention.
