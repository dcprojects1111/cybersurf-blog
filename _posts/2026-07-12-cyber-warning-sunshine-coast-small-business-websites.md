  # Aussie Cyber Centre Warns of Attack Campaign Targeting Small Business Websites

**If your business has a website — even a simple one — this warning from Australia's official cybersecurity agency is for you.**

The Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) has just issued an alert about a worldwide hacking campaign that's specifically targeting small business websites built on popular content management systems. If you're a tradie, salon owner, or accommodation host on the Sunshine Coast running a website you set up years ago (or paid someone to build), your site could already be compromised without you knowing.

## What's Actually Happening?

Cybercriminals are running automated attacks against websites built on platforms like WordPress, Joomla, and similar systems. They're not targeting you specifically — they're casting a wide net and exploiting websites that haven't been updated or secured properly.

Think of it like this: if your website is a shopfront, these attackers are walking down every street in the world trying every door handle to see which ones are unlocked. Once they find one, they slip inside.

## Why Should Sunshine Coast Business Owners Care?

You might be thinking, "I'm just a landscaper" or "I only run a small café — why would anyone hack me?" 

Here's the truth: **hackers don't care what you do. They care that you have a website they can use.**

Once they're inside your site, they can:

- Redirect your customers to scam websites
- Steal contact details from your enquiry forms
- Inject malware that infects anyone who visits your site
- Hold your website hostage and demand a ransom
- Use your site to attack others (making you legally liable)
- Damage your reputation when Google blacklists you for malware

### A Real-World Example

Imagine you're an Airbnb host on the Sunshine Coast with a small direct-booking website to avoid platform fees. One day a couple arrives at your door for a booking you never received — because hackers redirected your booking form to steal their payment. Meanwhile, your actual website is serving up malware, and Google has removed you from search results entirely.

You've just lost immediate income, future bookings, and your online reputation in one hit.

## What Makes Your Website Vulnerable?

The ACSC warning specifically mentions outdated content management systems and plugins. For most small business owners, this means:

- **Your website hasn't been updated in months (or years)**. That WordPress site you had built in 2019? If nobody's been maintaining it, it's almost certainly vulnerable.

- **Plugins and add-ons are out of date**. Contact forms, photo galleries, booking systems — these all need regular updates to patch security holes.

- **Your hosting is basic**. Cheap shared hosting often has minimal security protections.

- **Nobody's monitoring it**. You'd notice if your physical shop was broken into. But a website compromise can go undetected for months.

## This Isn't About Big IT Budgets

Here's the good news: protecting yourself from this specific threat doesn't require enterprise-level IT security or a massive budget. But it does require you to take small business cyber security seriously.

For sole traders and micro-businesses on the Sunshine Coast, an SME data breach often starts with exactly this kind of website vulnerability. You don't need to become a tech expert — you just need to know your current risk level.
