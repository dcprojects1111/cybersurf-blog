  # Fake ChatGPT Sites Are Targeting Aussie Small Businesses With Malware

If you've been curious about ChatGPT and searched for a way to download it recently, you need to read this. Cybercriminals have created convincing fake download sites that are infecting both Windows and Mac computers with malware — and small business owners across the Sunshine Coast are particularly vulnerable.

## What's Actually Happening?

ChatGPT has become incredibly popular over the past year, and scammers know it. They've set up fake websites that look legitimate, offering a "downloadable version" of ChatGPT. When unsuspecting users click the download button, they're actually installing malware designed to steal passwords, banking details, and business data.

What makes this threat particularly nasty is that the criminals have created separate versions of the malware for Windows and Mac users. So whether you're running your business from a PC in your home office or managing your bookings on a MacBook at your café, you're at risk.

## Why Small Business Owners Are Prime Targets

If you're a tradie, salon owner, or Airbnb host running everything from your phone and laptop, you might think you're too small to be targeted. Unfortunately, that's exactly why you're attractive to cybercriminals.

Consider this scenario: A local electrician sees a Facebook post about using AI to write quotes faster. He searches "download ChatGPT" on Google, clicks what looks like an official site, and installs the program. Within hours, malware is quietly harvesting his passwords. By the end of the week, his Xero account has been compromised, and fraudulent invoices are being sent to his clients.

This isn't hypothetical. Cyber security experts on the Sunshine Coast are seeing more SME data breach incidents linked to fake software downloads than ever before.

## How the Scam Works

Here's what makes these fake sites so dangerous:

**They look completely legitimate**
- Professional design that mimics real tech company websites
- Convincing domain names that look almost identical to the real thing
- Fake testimonials and trust badges

**They exploit your trust**
- You're genuinely trying to improve your business
- You've heard ChatGPT is free (which it is — online)
- The download seems like a convenient shortcut

**The malware works silently**
- No obvious warning signs your computer is infected
- Runs in the background stealing login credentials
- Can access your business banking, invoicing software, and customer data

## The Real Thing Doesn't Need Downloading

Here's what every small business owner needs to know: **ChatGPT doesn't require a download**. The legitimate service runs entirely through your web browser at openai.com or through the official mobile app from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store.

If any website is offering a downloadable desktop version of ChatGPT, it's a scam. Full stop.

## Protecting Your Business

Small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated. Here are the essentials:

**Before downloading anything:**
- Search for the official website directly (don't click ads in search results)
- Check if the software actually needs to be downloaded
- Look for reviews from trusted Australian tech sources

**General security habits:**
- Never download software from search engine ads
- Keep your computer and phone updated
- Use different passwords for different accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication on your banking and business apps
