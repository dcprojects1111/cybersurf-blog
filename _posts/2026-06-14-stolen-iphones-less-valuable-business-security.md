# Stolen iPhones Are About to Become a Lot Less Valuable — And What That Means for Your Business

Apple and London's Metropolitan Police have teamed up on something quietly brilliant: making stolen iPhones essentially worthless to thieves. The idea is simple — if a phone is reported stolen, it becomes much harder to reset, resell, or profit from. It's a smart move that could ripple out beyond London, and while it might not seem like front-page news for your Sunshine Coast café or plumbing business, there's a useful thread to pull on here.

## Does This Actually Affect Your Business?

Let's be honest — not directly. Unless you're in the business of selling second-hand phones or you've got a fleet of company iPhones that walk off job sites, this won't change your day-to-day. But here's why it still matters: **it's part of a bigger shift in how we think about device security, especially when those devices hold your business data.**

If your phone contains client details, invoice records, business banking apps, or email access — and let's face it, most do — then what happens when it goes missing is a real problem. It's not just the cost of replacing the handset. It's the risk of someone accessing your customer information, your accounts, or sensitive conversations. That's when a lost phone becomes a potential SME data breach, and that's a headache no one needs.

## What You Can Do Right Now

You don't need to wait for Apple or the police to protect your business. Here are two simple, practical steps you can take this week — no IT degree required.

### Turn on "Find My" and Activation Lock

If you've got an iPhone (or iPad), make sure **Find My** is switched on. This is Apple's built-in tracking and lock-down feature. If your phone goes missing, you can remotely lock it, wipe it, or track where it is. It also triggers something called Activation Lock, which stops anyone else from setting it up as their own device — even if they factory reset it.

Think of it like a deadbolt on your front door. It won't stop every break-in, but it makes your place a lot less appealing to someone looking for an easy target.

**How to check:**

- Open Settings
- Tap your name at the top
- Tap **Find My**
- Make sure **Find My iPhone** is turned on

### Use a Passcode (and Make It a Good One)

This one sounds obvious, but you'd be surprised. A passcode is your first line of defence. If your phone gets nicked from your ute or left behind at a job, a strong passcode buys you time to remotely wipe it before anyone gets in.

Avoid birthdates, "1234", or your business name. A six-digit PIN is better than four. A proper password or Face ID is even better.

## The Bigger Picture: Devices Are Doors

Here's the thing — whether you're running a physio clinic in Noosa or a shopfitting business in Caloundra, your phone is a door into your business systems. Treating it like just another tool in the van is a mistake. A basic IT security audit would flag this stuff immediately, but most small businesses haven't had one. And that's fine — now you know where to start.

The Apple-Met Police initiative is a reminder that security doesn't have to be complicated. Small, simple steps — like locking down your devices — go a long way toward keeping your business data safe and your stress levels low.
