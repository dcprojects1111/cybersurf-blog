  # Is Your Business Email Already Compromised? What Sunshine Coast Small Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running your tradie business, salon, or Airbnb from your phone and email, there's something you need to know: cybercriminals are actively targeting small business email accounts, and you might not even realise yours has been compromised.

Recent cybersecurity reports show that business email compromise remains one of the fastest-growing threats to Australian SMEs. Unlike the obvious ransomware attacks you hear about on the news, email compromise is silent, sneaky, and can go undetected for months while criminals quietly gather information or set up elaborate scams.

## What Is Business Email Compromise?

Business email compromise (BEC) happens when a cybercriminal gains access to your business email account. They don't just read your emails — they study them. They learn how you communicate, who your customers are, when you send invoices, and how you normally ask for payments.

Once they understand your business patterns, they strike. This might mean:

- Sending fake invoices to your customers that look exactly like yours, but with their bank details instead
- Intercepting real invoices and changing the payment details before your customer sees them
- Contacting your suppliers pretending to be you and updating payment information
- Accessing sensitive customer data stored in your emails
- Using your email account to launch attacks on your customers and contacts

## Why Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Are Prime Targets

You might think cybercriminals only target big corporations, but that's exactly what makes small businesses vulnerable. Sole traders and micro-businesses rarely have IT security audits, often use simple passwords, and typically don't have multi-factor authentication set up.

Imagine you're an electrician who's just finished a $15,000 rewiring job. You send the invoice from your compromised email account — but the criminal has already set up a filter that copies all your outgoing invoices. Minutes later, they send a follow-up email from your account saying "Sorry, that invoice had the wrong bank details — please use these ones instead." Your customer pays the fake account, and by the time anyone realises what's happened, the money is long gone.

Or consider a local Airbnb host whose email gets compromised. The criminal now has access to guest bookings, personal information, copies of driver's licences and passports, credit card details, and booking confirmations. The privacy breach alone could result in serious legal consequences, not to mention the reputational damage when guests find out their information has been exposed.

## The Warning Signs You Might Miss

Business email compromise is designed to be invisible, but there are subtle signs:

- Customers mentioning they haven't received invoices you definitely sent
- Unusual sent items in your email that you don't remember sending
- Suppliers or customers querying bank details you supposedly changed
- Your email sending messages when you're asleep or away
- Strange rules or filters appearing in your email settings
- Password reset emails you didn't request

The problem is, when you're juggling quotes, managing bookings, ordering supplies, and actually doing the work, these small oddities are easy to dismiss or overlook entirely.

## Protecting Your Sunshine Coast Small Business

Small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. Here are the essentials:

- **Enable multi-factor authentication** on your email account immediately
- **Use strong, unique passwords** for each business account
- **Be suspicious of unexpected payment changes** — always verify by phone using a number you already have
- **Regularly check your sent folder** for emails you didn't send
- **Review email forwarding rules** and filters in your settings

An IT security audit might sound like corporate overkill, but even basic checks can reveal whether your business email has already been compromised and is being monitored by criminals right now.
