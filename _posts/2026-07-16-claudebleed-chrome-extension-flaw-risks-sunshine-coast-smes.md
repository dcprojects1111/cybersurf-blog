  # Chrome Extension Flaw Puts Your Business Gmail at Risk: What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running your Sunshine Coast business from your phone and laptop — managing bookings, sending quotes, or chasing invoices through Gmail — this security flaw hits close to home.

A newly discovered vulnerability called "ClaudeBleed" shows how dodgy Chrome extensions can piggyback off legitimate tools to access your Gmail account, even if you'd never knowingly give them permission. For sole traders and micro-businesses without IT support, this is the kind of threat that can slip under the radar until it's too late.

## What's Actually Happening?

Claude for Chrome is a popular AI assistant extension that many business owners use to help draft emails, summarise documents, or manage their inbox more efficiently. The problem? Security researchers found that malicious extensions installed on the same browser can exploit Claude's permissions to secretly access your Gmail.

Think of it like this: you've given your trusted employee (Claude) the keys to your filing cabinet. But a dodgy contractor figures out they can walk in behind your employee and photocopy everything while you're not looking.

The vulnerability means that if you've installed any suspicious Chrome extensions — even ones that seem harmless — they could potentially:

- Read your emails and client communications
- Access quotes, invoices, and financial information
- Steal customer data and contact lists
- Monitor your business correspondence in real-time

## Why This Matters for Your Sunshine Coast Business

Let's make this real. Imagine you're an electrician on the Sunshine Coast with 20 years of client emails in Gmail. Quotes, job details, customer addresses, payment information — all sitting there. One rogue extension you downloaded months ago (maybe it promised to help track your jobs or manage your calendar) is now quietly harvesting that data.

Or picture this: you run a small accommodation business in Noosa. Your Gmail has booking confirmations, guest details, payment receipts, and property access codes. A compromised extension could hand all of that to criminals in minutes.

This isn't about having the latest firewall or a dedicated IT team. This is about the everyday tools you rely on becoming a back door into your business.

## The Bigger Picture: SME Data Breach Risks

Small business cyber security isn't just about big, headline-grabbing ransomware attacks. Most SME data breaches happen quietly, through exactly this kind of vulnerability — legitimate tools being exploited because small business owners don't have time to stay on top of every security update.

When you're flat out running your business, who has time to audit their Chrome extensions or think about cyber security on the Sunshine Coast? But the reality is, criminals specifically target sole traders and micro-businesses because they know you're stretched thin and likely don't have formal IT security measures in place.

## What You Should Do Right Now

Don't panic, but don't ignore this either. Here's your action list:

- **Review your Chrome extensions today** — remove anything you don't actively use or don't remember installing
- **Check which extensions have access to Gmail** — go to Chrome settings, then Extensions, and review permissions
- **Be suspicious of extensions that ask for broad permissions** — especially ones that want to "read and change all your data"
- **Use strong, unique passwords** — especially for your email, which is the gateway to everything else
- **Enable two-factor authentication** on your Gmail account if you haven't already
