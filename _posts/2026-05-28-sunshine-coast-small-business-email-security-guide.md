  # Your Business Email Just Became a Cybercriminal's Favourite Tool

If you run a small business on the Sunshine Coast, there's a good chance you're reading this on the same phone you use to manage client bookings, send invoices, and handle supplier payments. And there's an even better chance you've never given much thought to whether your email account could be used against you.

Unfortunately, cybercriminals have given it plenty of thought.

## What's Actually Happening Right Now

Across Australia, small business owners are discovering their email accounts have been quietly compromised — not to steal money directly, but to use their trusted email address as a weapon against their own clients and suppliers.

Here's how it works: a tradie receives what looks like a legitimate invoice from their regular supplier. Same email address, same logo, even the same casual sign-off. The only difference? The bank account details have been changed. By the time anyone notices, thousands of dollars have vanished into an account that's impossible to trace.

This isn't a sophisticated hack requiring technical expertise. In most cases, cybercriminals simply guess weak passwords or trick business owners into clicking a dodgy link. Once they're in, they sit quietly in your inbox, watching, learning your patterns, and waiting for the right moment to strike.

## Why Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Are Prime Targets

If you're thinking "my business is too small to be a target," you're not alone — but you're also exactly who these criminals are counting on.

**Small businesses are attractive targets because:**

- Most operate without IT security audits or professional cyber security support
- Business banking and client communications happen on personal devices
- Passwords are often reused across multiple platforms
- There's rarely any monitoring in place to detect unusual account activity
- The stakes are high enough to be profitable, but security is typically minimal

Consider this scenario: you're a local electrician who's just quoted $8,500 for a residential rewiring job. The client accepts and asks for payment details. A cybercriminal who's been monitoring your email swoops in, sends a near-identical message from your compromised account with their bank details, and intercepts the payment before you even know what's happened.

## The Real Cost of an SME Data Breach

When we talk about small business cyber security on the Sunshine Coast, we're not just talking about losing money — though that's painful enough.

**An email compromise can cost you:**

- Client trust and reputation that took years to build
- Liability for misdirected payments (yes, you may still be responsible)
- Time spent dealing with banks, police, and distressed clients
- Lost business while you're firefighting the breach
- Stress and anxiety that affects your health and family life

For a café owner managing thin margins, or an Airbnb host relying on consistent bookings, even a single incident can be financially devastating.

## What You Can Do Today

The good news? You don't need to become a cybersecurity expert or hire expensive IT consultants to protect your business.

**Start with these practical steps:**

- Use unique, strong passwords for every business account (consider a password manager)
- Turn on two-factor authentication wherever it's offered
- Be suspicious of unexpected requests to change payment details — always verify by phone
- Check your sent folder regularly for emails you didn't send
- Keep your phone and computer software up to date
