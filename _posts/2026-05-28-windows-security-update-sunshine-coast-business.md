# Windows Security Update: What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, your Windows PC is about to face an important security deadline. Microsoft has announced they're replacing old Secure Boot certificates, with changes taking effect in June 2026. While this might sound like technical jargon, it could have real implications for your business operations—whether you're running a plumbing company, accounting practice, or local café.

## What's Actually Happening?

Microsoft is updating the security certificates that protect your Windows computer from malicious software during the startup process. Think of these certificates like the locks on your business premises—over time, old locks become easier to pick, so you need to upgrade them.

The deadline is June 2026, and while that might seem far away, older computers may not handle this transition smoothly. If your PC fails to update properly, it could miss out on crucial future security protections.

You might be thinking, "I'm just a small business—why would hackers target me?" Unfortunately, small to medium enterprises (SMEs) are increasingly attractive targets for cybercriminals. Here's why this update affects you:

## Will Your Computer Be Affected?

Generally, computers purchased within the last five years should handle this update without issues. However, older machines—particularly those running Windows 10 on aging hardware—might struggle.

**Warning signs your PC might be vulnerable:**
- Your computer is more than 6-7 years old
- You've received messages about hardware compatibility issues
- Your business is still using Windows 10 (which reaches end-of-life in October 2025)
- You've postponed system updates due to concerns about disruption
