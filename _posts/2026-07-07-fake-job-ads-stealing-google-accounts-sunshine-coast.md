  # Fake Job Ads Are Now Being Used to Steal Google Accounts on the Sunshine Coast

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast and occasionally browse LinkedIn or job boards—even just to keep an eye on what's out there or maybe hire someone down the track—there's a sophisticated scam you need to know about.

Cybercriminals are creating fake job interviews with household names like Adobe, Netflix, Coca-Cola, and OpenAI to steal Google account logins. And while this might sound like something that only happens to big corporates, the reality is that small business owners are prime targets.

## Why This Matters for Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

Here's the thing: most of us wear multiple hats. You might be running your café's Instagram account, managing bookings through Google Calendar, handling invoices in Xero or MYOB, and coordinating staff rosters—all from the same Google account you use personally.

Imagine this scenario: You're an accountant in Maroochydore who's put out feelers for a junior bookkeeper. You receive what looks like a genuine LinkedIn message from a recruitment agency representing a major software company. They want to "interview" you for a consulting opportunity. The email looks professional. The branding is spot-on. You click the link, enter your Google login details on what appears to be a standard sign-in page, and... you've just handed over the keys to your entire business.

Within hours, scammers could:

- Lock you out of your own email and Google Drive
- Access client information and financial records
- Send invoicing emails to your customers asking them to pay into fraudulent accounts
- Impersonate you to your staff, suppliers, or clients
- Hijack any other accounts linked to that email address

For a tradie, Airbnb host, or retail shop owner with no IT support, this can be business-ending.

## How the Scam Actually Works

The phishing campaign is clever because it doesn't just blast out dodgy emails. It targets people who might realistically be job hunting or open to new opportunities—often marketing professionals, but also business owners exploring partnerships or contract work.

Here's what typically happens:

- You receive a message on LinkedIn, email, or even WhatsApp from someone claiming to represent a well-known brand
- The message invites you to a "virtual interview" or asks you to complete an online assessment
- You're sent a link to what looks like a legitimate Google login page
- Once you enter your username and password, the scammers capture it in real time
- They immediately use it to access your account, change recovery details, and lock you out

The fake login pages are very convincing. They use real logos, proper colours, and even mimic the exact layout of a genuine Google sign-in screen.

## What You Can Do Right Now

You don't need to become a cyber security expert to protect yourself, but a few simple habits go a long way:

- **Be suspicious of unsolicited job offers**, especially if they come out of the blue or seem too good to be true
- **Never click login links in emails or messages**—instead, go directly to the website by typing it into your browser
- **Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)** for your Google account and any business apps. Even if someone gets your password, they can't get in without the second code
- **Check the URL carefully** before entering any login details. Scammers often use addresses like "google-login.verify-account.com" instead of the real "accounts.google.com"
