  # New Phishing Kit Targets Microsoft Accounts: What Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Need to Know

If you're running your business from your phone or laptop using Microsoft 365 — Outlook for emails, Teams for client chats, OneDrive for storing quotes and invoices — there's a new cyber threat you should know about.

The FBI has recently warned about a sophisticated phishing kit called Kali365 that's giving cybercriminals long-term access to Microsoft accounts. What makes this particularly concerning for small business owners on the Sunshine Coast is that it bypasses the very security measure most of us rely on: multi-factor authentication (MFA).

## What Is This Kali365 Threat?

Kali365 is a phishing toolkit that cybercriminals are using to steal Microsoft login credentials. Unlike older phishing scams that might get caught by spam filters or stopped by two-factor authentication, this new kit is specifically designed to get around those protections.

Here's what makes it dangerous:

- It tricks users into entering their login details on fake Microsoft pages that look completely legitimate
- It captures your multi-factor authentication codes in real-time
- It gives attackers ongoing access to your account, not just a one-time entry
- It can access Outlook emails, Teams conversations, and OneDrive files

## Why This Matters for Sunshine Coast Tradies and Small Business Owners

Imagine you're an electrician with three employees. You use Outlook to send quotes, OneDrive to store job schedules and client information, and Teams to coordinate with your team. One morning, you click what looks like a legitimate Microsoft security alert asking you to verify your login.

Within hours, a cybercriminal has access to:

- Your client contact database
- Banking details stored in old emails
- Your upcoming job schedule
- Confidential customer information
- Your ability to send emails that appear to come from you

They could lock you out, send fake invoices to your clients requesting payment to a different bank account, or hold your data ransom. For a sole trader or micro-business with no IT support, this could shut down operations for days or even weeks.

## How the Attack Works

The Kali365 phishing kit works by creating convincing fake login pages that look identical to Microsoft's real ones. These typically arrive via:

- Email alerts claiming there's a security issue with your account
- Messages warning that your storage is full
- Notifications about missed Teams messages
- Alerts about suspicious login attempts (ironically)

When you enter your username, password, and even your MFA code, the system captures everything and immediately uses it to access your real account before the code expires.

## Protecting Your Small Business From This Threat

The challenge with small business cyber security on the Sunshine Coast is that most sole traders and micro-businesses don't have dedicated IT support. You're doing everything yourself, often from your phone between jobs or during quiet moments at the shop.

Here are practical steps you can take today:

**Be suspicious of login requests:**
- Don't click links in emails asking you to log in to Microsoft
- Instead, go directly to outlook.com or office.com by typing the address yourself
- Microsoft will never ask you to verify your account via email link

**Check the web address carefully:**
- Real Microsoft login pages always have microsoft.com in the URL
- Fake pages might use microsoft-security.net or similar variations
- Look for the padlock icon, but know that fake sites can have them too

**Use Microsoft Authenticator if possible:**
- The Authenticator app provides stronger protection than SMS codes
- It can show you where login attempts are coming from
- It asks you to approve sign-ins rather than entering codes

**Watch for unusual account activity:**
- Strange sent emails you didn't write
- Files in OneDrive you don't recognise
- Login alerts from unfamiliar locations

The concerning thing about this type of SME data breach is that you might not notice immediately. Attackers often gain access and quietly monitor accounts for weeks, learning about your business, your clients, and your payment processes before they strike.
