  # Your Business Email Could Be Putting Client Money at Risk — Here's What Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Need to Know

If you're running your business from your phone — managing bookings, sending invoices, or coordinating jobs through email — this one's for you. A new wave of cybercriminals is targeting everyday business email accounts, and they're getting smarter about it.

You don't need to be a tech expert to protect yourself, but you do need to understand what's happening and why it matters for your business here on the Sunshine Coast.

## What's Actually Going On?

Hackers are increasingly focused on compromising business email accounts. Once they're in, they don't just send spam. They watch, learn your communication style, and wait for the right moment to strike.

Here's a real-world scenario: imagine you're a builder who's just finished a $15,000 renovation job. You email the final invoice to your client. But unknown to you, a cybercriminal has access to your email. They intercept your invoice, create a near-identical copy with their own bank details, and send it from your account. Your client pays — but the money goes straight to the scammer.

By the time anyone realizes what's happened, the money's gone, your client relationship is damaged, and you're left sorting through a nightmare of police reports and disputes.

## Why Small Businesses Are Being Targeted

You might think, "I'm just a gardener" or "I only run a small café — why would anyone target me?" That's exactly what makes you vulnerable.

**Cybercriminals know that:**

- Small business owners on the Sunshine Coast rarely have IT security audits or dedicated tech support
- Most sole traders and micro-businesses use simple, reused passwords
- Business owners trust their email completely and don't expect it could be compromised
- There's real money flowing through these accounts — invoices, supplier payments, customer transactions

An Airbnb host managing multiple properties, for instance, has access to guest payment information and booking systems. A hairdresser with a digital booking platform holds client contact details and payment data. These are valuable targets for a patient cybercriminal.

## The Warning Signs Your Email Might Be Compromised

**Watch out for these red flags:**

- Clients or suppliers mentioning emails you never sent
- Sent items in your email that you don't recognize
- Password reset emails you didn't request
- Being suddenly locked out of your account
- Unusual login notifications from locations you've never visited

The tricky part? Often there are no warning signs at all. Sophisticated attacks are designed to be invisible.

## What This Means for SME Data Breach Risks

When we talk about small business cyber security, email compromise is one of the most common — and costly — threats facing Sunshine Coast businesses today.

An SME data breach doesn't always mean your customer database was stolen (though that can happen too). Sometimes it's as simple as a hacker gaining access to your everyday business communications and using that access to redirect payments, impersonate you to clients, or gather information for more targeted attacks later.

## Practical Steps You Can Take Right Now

**You don't need a computer science degree to protect your business. Start here:**

- Turn on two-factor authentication for your email account (yes, even your Gmail)
- Use unique passwords for different accounts — a password manager can help
- Be suspicious of any last-minute changes to payment details, even if the email looks legitimate
- Call to confirm any unusual payment requests, even from people you know
- Keep your phone and apps updated with the latest security patches
