  # Email Scams Are Getting Smarter — Is Your Sunshine Coast Business Ready?

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, chances are your email inbox is the engine room of everything you do. Quotes, bookings, invoices, supplier orders — it all flows through your phone or laptop. But here's something that doesn't get talked about enough: email-based scams have evolved, and the old advice about "just don't click dodgy links" isn't enough anymore.

## The New Wave of Email Attacks Targeting Small Business Owners

Cyber criminals aren't sending out clumsy spam anymore. They're studying how real businesses communicate, then mimicking it with frightening accuracy. These modern email attacks are often called **phishing** or **business email compromise (BEC)**, and they're designed to trick you into handing over money, passwords, or access to your systems.

Imagine you're a builder coordinating a job with a supplier you've used for years. You get an email that looks identical to their usual invoices — same logo, same tone, same email signature — but the bank account details have been changed. You pay the invoice, and the money disappears. That's BEC in action, and it's happening to tradies, retail shop owners, and service providers all over Australia.

Or picture this: you run a busy café on the Sunshine Coast. One morning, you can't log into your point-of-sale system. Your "password has been changed," the system says. Turns out, a scammer got into your email account overnight, reset your passwords, and now they're locking you out of the tools you need to open for business. That's an **account takeover**, and it can paralyse your operation in minutes.

## Why Traditional Email Security Isn't Cutting It

Most of us rely on the basic spam filter that comes with Gmail or Outlook. And while those filters catch some threats, they're not built to spot the sophisticated stuff — emails that come from legitimate-looking addresses, reference real projects or clients, and use language that sounds exactly like your normal correspondence.

The cybersecurity industry is now turning to something called **behavioural AI** — technology that learns what normal email behaviour looks like for your business, then flags anything unusual. If someone suddenly sends an invoice with different bank details, or your account starts sending emails at 3 a.m., the system notices.

But here's the reality: that kind of protection is still out of reach for most sole traders and micro-businesses. You don't have an IT team. You're not attending webinars on cyber security. You're just trying to keep the lights on and serve your customers.

## What Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Can Do Right Now

You don't need to become a cybersecurity expert, but you do need to take this seriously. Here are a few practical steps:

**Protect your email account:**

- Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) for your email — it's usually free and takes five minutes
- Use a strong, unique password (not the same one you use everywhere else)
- Be suspicious of any email asking you to click a link, download something, or change payment details — even if it looks legit

**Watch for the warning signs:**

- Unexpected password reset emails
- Invoices or payment requests that feel slightly "off"
- Emails from known contacts that don't quite sound like them

**Get a health check:**

- Just like you'd service your ute or get your books reviewed by your accountant, your digital systems need a once-over too. An **IT security audit** doesn't have to be expensive or complicated — but it can save you thousands in losses and downtime.
