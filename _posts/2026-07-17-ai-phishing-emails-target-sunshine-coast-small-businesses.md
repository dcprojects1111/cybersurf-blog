  # Cybercriminals Are Now Using AI to Write More Convincing Phishing Emails — Here's What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you've noticed scam emails getting harder to spot lately, you're not alone. Cybercriminals have started using artificial intelligence to write phishing messages that look and sound surprisingly legitimate — and small businesses across the Sunshine Coast are increasingly in the firing line.

For sole traders and micro-businesses who run everything from their phone and don't have IT support, this shift represents a genuine threat that's worth understanding and preparing for.

## What's Changed With AI-Powered Phishing?

Traditional phishing emails were often easy to identify. They had spelling mistakes, awkward phrasing, and claims that didn't quite add up. You could usually spot them a mile away.

Now, criminals are using the same AI writing tools that businesses use for marketing to craft emails that:

- Contain perfect spelling and grammar
- Use convincing Australian business language
- Reference real company names and services you actually use
- Create a genuine sense of urgency without obvious red flags
- Personalise messages based on publicly available information about you

For a landscaper juggling quotes on their phone between jobs, or a café owner checking emails during the morning rush, these polished scam messages can be almost impossible to distinguish from legitimate correspondence.

## A Real-World Example: The Xero Email That Wasn't

Imagine you're an electrician wrapping up a job in Buderim when an email arrives on your phone. It looks exactly like it's from Xero, your accounting software. The logo's right, the formatting matches, and it's telling you there's an urgent issue with a direct debit that needs your attention.

You click the link, enter your login details to "verify your account," and move on with your day.

Within hours, someone on the other side of the world now has access to your Xero account — your client list, your banking details, your invoices, and your financial records. This is exactly how modern AI-powered phishing works, and it's affecting small business cyber security across Australia every single day.

## Why Small Businesses Are Being Targeted

Criminals know that sole traders and micro-businesses on the Sunshine Coast typically:

- Don't have dedicated IT security staff
- Manage multiple online accounts and subscriptions
- Make quick decisions on their phones throughout the day
- Often lack formal cyber security training
- May not have recovery plans if something goes wrong

This makes small businesses easier targets than large corporations with security teams — yet small businesses often have just as much valuable data.

## How to Protect Your Business

The good news is that awareness is your best defence. Here's what you can do starting today:

**Slow down before clicking**
- If an email creates urgency, that's your cue to pause, not rush
- Verify requests by contacting the company directly using contact details you find yourself

**Check the sender carefully**
- Look at the actual email address, not just the display name
- Watch for tiny variations in spelling (like "support@xer0.com" instead of "xero.com")

**Enable two-factor authentication**
- Add this extra security layer to your critical accounts
- It stops criminals even if they get your password

**Keep software updated**
- Enable automatic updates on your phone and computer
- These updates patch security vulnerabilities criminals exploit
