  ## WhatsApp's New Usernames: What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, chances are WhatsApp is one of your main tools for staying in touch with customers, suppliers, and maybe even your team. Whether you're confirming a plumbing job in Noosa, sending paint colour options to a client in Maroochydore, or coordinating cleaners between Airbnb bookings, WhatsApp keeps things moving.

But here's something you might have missed: WhatsApp has just rolled out a new username feature designed to protect your privacy. And while that sounds like good news, there's a catch that could actually put your business at risk if you're not careful.

## Why WhatsApp Introduced Usernames

Until now, anyone you chatted with on WhatsApp could see your phone number. For business owners, that's been a double-edged sword. Your number is often your shopfront—printed on utes, business cards, and Facebook pages—but it's also your personal contact, the one linked to your banking apps, your myGov account, and everything else that keeps your business running.

WhatsApp's new username feature lets you create a handle (like @SunnyCoastPlumbing) that people can use to contact you instead of sharing your actual phone number. It's a privacy upgrade that makes sense, especially as cyber threats targeting small businesses continue to rise across Australia.

## The Privacy Problem You Might Create Yourself

Here's where good intentions can go wrong. When choosing your WhatsApp username, you might be tempted to use something obvious—your full name, your business name, your ABN, or even your email address. That feels professional, right?

Actually, it can be a cyber security risk.

**Here's why:**

- **It makes you easier to target.** Scammers and hackers build profiles on people. If your username is JohnSmithElec or MiaBeautyNambour, it's incredibly easy to figure out who you are, what you do, and how to craft a convincing scam message.
- **It connects the dots across platforms.** If you use the same username on Instagram, Facebook, or your booking system, a cybercriminal can piece together your digital footprint—your suppliers, your customers, even your bank.
- **It gives away too much.** A username that includes your suburb, your business type, or your full identity makes social engineering attacks much easier. That's when someone pretends to be a tradie, a supplier, or even a customer to trick you into handing over access or money.

## Real-World Example: The Airbnb Host in Coolum

Imagine you're managing a short-stay property in Coolum. You use WhatsApp to communicate with guests, cleaners, and your linen supplier. You set your username as @CoolumBeachStays because it's memorable and matches your listing name.

A scammer finds your listing online, sees your WhatsApp username, and sends you a message pretending to be from Airbnb support. They know your property name, your location, and your contact method. The message looks legitimate. They ask you to "verify your account" by clicking a link. You do—and suddenly, they've got access to your hosting account, your calendar, your payouts, and your guest data.

That's not a scare story. It's the kind of SME data breach happening right now to small business owners who don't have IT security audit systems in place.

## How to Choose a Safer WhatsApp Username

You don't need to be a tech expert to make smarter choices. Here's what to do:

- **Avoid using your full name or business name**
- **Don't include your location or trade**
- **Keep it generic and unrelated to your business identity**
- **Don't reuse usernames from other platforms**
- **Think of it like a password—functional, not promotional**

Your WhatsApp username isn't a marketing tool. It's a privacy shield. Treat it that way.
