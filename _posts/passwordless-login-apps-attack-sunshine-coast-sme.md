  # Passwordless Login Apps Under Attack: What Sunshine Coast Small Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running your business from your phone—managing bookings, sending invoices, or communicating with customers—you've probably embraced "passwordless" login apps. They're convenient, quick, and promised to be more secure than remembering dozens of passwords. But cybercriminals have found a way in, and small businesses on the Sunshine Coast need to pay attention.

## What's Happening with Passwordless Apps?

Passwordless authentication apps like Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator, and similar tools let you log in with a fingerprint, face scan, or a code sent to your phone instead of typing a password. They're everywhere now—from your MYOB account to your social media business pages.

Recently, security researchers have identified a growing wave of attacks targeting these systems. Hackers are exploiting weaknesses in how these apps communicate with cloud services, intercepting login requests, and gaining access to business accounts without ever needing your actual password.

## How These Attacks Actually Work

You don't need to understand all the technical details, but here's the basics:

**The "Man-in-the-Middle" Trick**

Hackers create fake login pages that look identical to the real thing. When you try to log in, they capture your authentication request in real-time and use it to access your account before the session expires—all while you think you've just logged in normally.

**SIM Swapping**

Criminals convince your mobile provider to transfer your number to their device. Once they control your number, they can intercept authentication codes sent via SMS and take over your accounts.

**Malware on Your Device**

Dodgy apps or links can install software on your phone that monitors your authentication apps and steals session tokens—the digital keys that prove you're you.

## Protecting Your Business: Practical Steps

The good news? You can significantly reduce your risk with a few straightforward actions:

- **Use hardware security keys** where possible for your most important accounts (banking, accounting software)
- **Enable additional verification** beyond just the authentication app
- **Keep your phone's operating system updated**—those annoying update reminders are actually security patches
- **Be suspicious of login requests you didn't initiate**—if your authenticator app asks you to approve a login you didn't start, decline it immediately
- **Use different authentication methods for different account types**—don't rely solely on SMS codes
