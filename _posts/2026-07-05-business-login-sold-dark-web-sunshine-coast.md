  # Has Your Business Login Been Sold on the Dark Web? What Sunshine Coast Small Business Owners Need to Know

If you run a small business on the Sunshine Coast—whether you're an electrician, a café owner, or managing a couple of holiday rentals—there's a good chance you've never given much thought to cyber security. You're busy enough keeping customers happy and the bills paid. But here's something that might surprise you: right now, someone could be selling access to your business accounts on the dark web, and you'd have no idea.

## The Threat You Can't See

Recent investigations have revealed a thriving underground marketplace where criminals buy and sell stolen login credentials for thousands of dollars. These aren't just passwords for big corporations—they're targeting small businesses like yours. The reason? Small businesses on the Sunshine Coast and elsewhere rarely have IT security measures in place, making them easy targets.

Think about all the apps and systems you use to run your business:

- Your accounting software (Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks)
- Booking and scheduling platforms
- Your business banking app
- Email accounts
- Social media business pages
- Supplier ordering systems

Each one of these is a potential entry point for cybercriminals.

## How It Actually Happens

Let's say you're a landscaper who uses the same password for your email and your invoicing software. You click a link in what looks like a legitimate email—maybe it appears to be from Australia Post or your bank. You enter your details. Within minutes, criminals have captured that password.

They try it across multiple platforms. Suddenly they've got access to your client list, your banking details, and your business communications. Before you know it, they're sending invoices to your customers with their own bank details, or locking you out of your own accounts and demanding ransom.

This isn't a hypothetical scenario. It's happening to Sunshine Coast tradespeople, accommodation hosts, and shop owners regularly. Many don't even realise they've been compromised until a customer mentions they paid an invoice that never arrived, or they find they can't log into their own systems.

## Why Small Business Cyber Security Matters More Than Ever

Criminals specifically target small businesses because they know most sole traders and micro-businesses operate without proper IT security. You're doing everything from your phone, often using public Wi-Fi at the café down the road, and you've probably reused the same password across multiple accounts.

The financial impact of an SME data breach can be devastating. Beyond the immediate theft, there's the cost of:

- Lost business while you're locked out of your systems
- Damage to your reputation when clients' data is exposed
- Time spent recovering accounts and resetting everything
- Potential fines if customer information is compromised

## What You Can Do Right Now

The good news is that protecting yourself doesn't require becoming a tech expert. Here are some straightforward steps:

**Use unique passwords for every business account.** Yes, it's inconvenient, but it's also the single most effective thing you can do. Consider using a password manager app to keep track.

**Enable two-factor authentication** wherever it's offered. This means even if someone steals your password, they still can't access your account without a second code sent to your phone.

**Be suspicious of unexpected emails,** especially ones asking you to click links or confirm details. When in doubt, contact the company directly using a phone number you look up yourself.

**Keep your phone and apps updated.** Those annoying update notifications actually include important security patches.
