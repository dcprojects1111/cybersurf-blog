  # New Phishing Attack Targeting Small Businesses Through Fake Service Invoices

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, there's a new email scam doing the rounds that you need to know about. Cybercriminals are sending incredibly convincing fake invoices that look like they're from suppliers and services you actually use — and local businesses are already getting caught out.

## What's Happening Right Now

Scammers have stepped up their game. They're no longer sending obvious spam emails with poor grammar and dodgy logos. The latest phishing attacks use realistic-looking invoices from companies like Telstra, Xero, Office Works, and even your local trade suppliers.

Here's what makes this particularly nasty: these fake invoices often arrive right when you're expecting a bill. The amounts seem reasonable, the branding looks spot-on, and the payment details appear legitimate at first glance.

## How This Could Hit Your Business

Imagine you're a landscaper who's just finished a big job at Noosa. You're juggling quotes, ordering supplies, and managing your crew — all from your phone while you're on site. A Bunnings invoice arrives in your email for the timber and materials you ordered last week. You click to pay it without thinking twice. 

Except it wasn't from Bunnings. The money's gone to a scammer's account, and now you're out thousands of dollars *and* you still owe Bunnings for the actual materials.

Or picture this: you run a beauty salon in Maroochydore. You get an email that looks like it's from your booking software company saying your payment failed and you need to update your credit card details urgently or your account will be suspended. You can't afford to lose access to your appointment calendar, so you click the link and enter your card details. Within hours, fraudulent charges start appearing.

This isn't just about losing money on a fake invoice. When these scammers get your payment details or login credentials, they can:

* Access your banking and accounting software
* Lock you out of critical business systems
* Steal customer data from your records
* Use your email to scam your own clients
* Rack up fraudulent charges on your credit card

## Why Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Are Targets

Cybercriminals specifically target sole traders and micro-businesses because they know you're busy, you're often working from your phone, and you probably don't have IT support watching your back. 

Small business cyber security often takes a back seat when you're focused on actually running your business and serving customers. That's completely understandable — but it's exactly what these scammers count on.

SME data breaches don't just happen to big companies. In fact, small businesses on the Sunshine Coast are increasingly attractive targets because the cyber security measures are often minimal or non-existent.

## What You Can Do About It

The good news? You don't need to become a tech expert to protect yourself. Here are the basics:

* Always double-check payment details by calling the company directly — use the number from their official website, not the one in the email
* Look closely at email addresses: scammers use addresses like "account@tel5tra.com" instead of the real "telstra.com"
* Never click links in unexpected invoice emails — go directly to the company's website instead
* Set up two-factor authentication on your banking and business apps
* Be extra cautious when you're busy or distracted — that's when mistakes happen
