  # Is Your Business Software About to Get More Expensive? What Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Need to Know

If you run your business from your phone—managing bookings, invoices, or customer communications through subscription apps—you need to pay attention to what's happening in the software world right now. Major tech companies are quietly changing how they deliver their services, and it could affect the tools you rely on every day.

## The Shift Happening Right Now

Tech companies are increasingly moving their most powerful features away from simple monthly subscriptions and into "usage-based" pricing models. What does that mean for you? Instead of paying a flat $20 or $50 per month for unlimited access to your software, you might soon be paying based on how much you actually use it—and that cost can add up quickly if you're not paying attention.

This shift is happening across business software, from customer management systems to AI-powered booking tools and automated scheduling apps. The companies say it's temporary, but "temporary" in the tech world can last months—or become permanent without warning.

## Why This Matters for Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

Let's say you're a landscape gardener in Buderim who uses an app to generate quotes and manage your schedule. Or maybe you're running a short-stay accommodation in Noosa and rely on automated messaging to communicate with guests. If your software provider suddenly changes their pricing model:

- Your monthly costs could double or triple without warning
- You might lose access to features you've built your workflow around
- You could be forced to migrate to a new system at the worst possible time—right in the middle of peak season

Here's the bigger cybersecurity concern: when businesses are forced to suddenly change software providers, they often make rushed decisions. They skip the security checks, they don't properly transfer their data, and they accidentally leave sensitive customer information exposed in their old accounts.

## The Real Risk for Tradies and Service Businesses

Imagine this scenario: You're an electrician on the Sunshine Coast who's been using the same invoicing app for three years. It suddenly announces a pricing change that makes it unaffordable. You frantically sign up for a cheaper alternative, quickly import your customer list, and keep working.

But you forgot to properly close down the old account. Your old login credentials—probably the same email and password you use for other services—are still active. Customer phone numbers, addresses, and payment history are still sitting there. If that account gets compromised, you've got an SME data breach on your hands.

This is exactly how small business cyber security incidents happen. Not from sophisticated hackers, but from rushed transitions and forgotten accounts.

## What You Should Do Now

**Audit your software subscriptions:**

- List every business app you pay for monthly
- Note which ones hold customer data
- Check if any have announced pricing changes recently

**Prepare for changes:**

- Don't wait until you're forced to switch
- Research alternatives before you need them
- Ensure you know how to export your data properly

**Protect your credentials:**

- Never reuse passwords across business apps
- Keep a secure record of all your logins
- Know which accounts have access to sensitive information
