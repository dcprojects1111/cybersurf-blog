## Why Email Compromise Matters for Your Business

When cybercriminals get access to your email, they're not just reading your messages. They're gaining a foothold into your entire business operation. Think about what's connected to your email account:

- Your invoicing and accounting software
- Customer booking systems
- Banking notifications and payment platforms
- Supplier communications and orders
- Cloud storage with business documents
- Social media business pages

For a landscaper on the Sunshine Coast, a compromised email could mean a criminal intercepts payment instructions from a client and redirects $15,000 to their own account. By the time you realise what's happened, the money is long gone and the client thinks they've already paid you.

## How Business Email Gets Compromised

You don't need to click on a suspicious link for your email to end up in the wrong hands. Here's what's actually happening:

**Data breaches from other services:** That free project management tool you signed up for three years ago? If they suffered a data breach and you used the same password for your email, criminals now have access.

**Credential stuffing attacks:** Hackers use automated tools to test millions of stolen username and password combinations across different platforms. If you've reused passwords—and let's be honest, most busy business owners have—your accounts are vulnerable.

**Weak passwords:** "Password123" or your business name plus the year might seem fine, but these are cracked in seconds by modern tools.

The scary part? This all happens silently. There's no warning, no notification. Criminals often sit quietly in compromised accounts for weeks, learning about your business, your clients, and your financial patterns before they strike.

## Warning Signs Your Email Might Be Compromised

While email breaches are often invisible, there are some red flags Sunshine Coast small business owners should watch for:

- Clients or suppliers mentioning they received unusual emails from you
- Password reset notifications you didn't request
- Sent items you don't remember sending
- Unexpected changes to your email forwarding rules
- Being suddenly locked out of your account
