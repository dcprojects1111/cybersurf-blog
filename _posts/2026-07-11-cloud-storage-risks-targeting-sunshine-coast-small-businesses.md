  # Cyber Criminals Are Targeting Small Businesses Through Cloud Storage Weaknesses

If you're running a tradie business, salon, café, or Airbnb on the Sunshine Coast, chances are you're using cloud storage like Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive every single day. You might store client details, invoices, quotes, before-and-after photos, or booking information there. It's convenient, accessible from your phone, and it just works.

But here's what most small business owners don't realise: cyber criminals are increasingly targeting cloud storage accounts because they know small businesses rarely have proper security in place.

## Why Cloud Storage Has Become a Prime Target

Recent reports from cybersecurity experts show a sharp increase in attacks specifically aimed at cloud storage platforms. The reason? They're a goldmine of business-critical information, and for small businesses, they're often protected by nothing more than a simple password.

Think about what you've got stored in the cloud right now. Customer phone numbers and addresses. Payment details. Business bank statements. Photos of clients' homes or properties. Supplier contracts. Tax records.

For a cyber criminal, that's everything they need to:

* Steal your clients' personal information and sell it
* Impersonate your business to scam your customers
* Lock you out and demand a ransom to get your data back
* Use your information to commit identity fraud

## A Real Risk for Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

Picture this: You're a landscape gardener with 15 years of client records, property access codes, and job photos stored in Google Drive. One morning, you wake up to find you're locked out. All your quotes for the week ahead are gone. Your client list is gone. Someone in another country now has access to your customers' home addresses and gate codes.

Or imagine you run a beauty salon in Maroochydore. Your cloud storage gets hacked, and suddenly your clients' names, phone numbers, and treatment history are being sold online. Not only have you lost their trust, but you may also be liable under privacy laws for not protecting their information properly.

These aren't scare tactics — they're scenarios playing out across Australia every week. And when it comes to cyber security on the Sunshine Coast, small businesses are particularly vulnerable because they assume they're "too small to be targeted."

## How These Attacks Actually Happen

You don't need to be specifically chosen by a hacker. Most cloud storage breaches happen through:

**Weak or reused passwords** — Using the same password across multiple accounts means one breach gives criminals access to everything

**Phishing emails** — A fake email that looks like it's from Google, Microsoft, or Dropbox tricks you into handing over your login details

**No two-factor authentication** — Without that second security step, a stolen password is all someone needs

**Shared access that's never revoked** — That apprentice who left two years ago? They might still have access to your business files

## What You Can Do Right Now

Small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. Here are three things you can do today:

* **Turn on two-factor authentication** for all your cloud storage accounts — it takes five minutes and blocks most attacks
* **Use unique, strong passwords** for each account (or better yet, use a password manager)
* **Review who has access** to your cloud storage and remove anyone who doesn't need it anymore
