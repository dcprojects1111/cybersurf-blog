  # Amazon Job Scam Texts Are Targeting Sunshine Coast Small Business Owners

If you've received a text message recently promising easy money working from home for Amazon, you're not alone — and you're being targeted by scammers. This sophisticated scam is making the rounds across Australia, and small business owners on the Sunshine Coast need to know what to look out for.

## What the Amazon Job Scam Looks Like

The scam starts simply enough: you receive an unexpected text message offering you a "work from home" opportunity with Amazon. The pitch is designed to sound perfect — high pay for minimal effort, flexible hours, and no experience required.

Here's what makes these messages so convincing:

- They use Amazon's trusted brand name to appear legitimate
- The promised work sounds simple — usually rating products, posting reviews, or handling basic online tasks
- They offer unusually high hourly rates, sometimes $30–$50 per hour or more
- They create urgency by suggesting limited positions are available
- The messages often include a link to a WhatsApp group or dodgy website

For a busy tradie juggling multiple job sites, or a café owner working long hours, the promise of some extra cash on the side can be genuinely tempting. That's exactly what the scammers are counting on.

## How the Scam Actually Works

Once you click the link or respond to the message, things escalate quickly. The scammers will typically:

- Ask you to complete a few small, paid "tasks" first — and they might even pay you a small amount to build trust
- Request your personal details including your driver's licence, bank account information, or Medicare number
- Eventually ask you to pay an "upfront fee" for training materials, software access, or to "process" higher-paying jobs
- Use your information to steal your identity or drain your bank accounts

Imagine you're a landscape gardener on the Sunshine Coast who's just landed a big job. You need to order supplies and equipment, and your cash flow is tight for a few weeks. That "easy money" text arrives at exactly the right time — or so it seems. You hand over your bank details, and within days your account is emptied. Now you can't pay your suppliers, and your business reputation is on the line.

## Why Small Business Owners Are Prime Targets

Scammers specifically target sole traders and micro-business owners because they know you:

- Often use personal devices for business
- Rarely have dedicated IT security or support
- Are time-poor and may not scrutinise messages carefully
- Handle your own finances and have direct access to business accounts
- May not have comprehensive cyber security sunshine coast protection in place

This makes SME data breach incidents increasingly common, even in our local area.

## How to Protect Yourself and Your Business

The good news is that protecting yourself from this type of scam is straightforward:

- **Delete unsolicited job offers immediately** — Amazon doesn't recruit via text message
- **Never click links in unexpected messages** — even if they look legitimate
- **Don't share personal or financial information** with anyone who contacts you out of the blue
- **Check the official company website** — if Amazon is hiring, it'll be on their actual careers page
- **Talk to other business owners** — share warnings in your local business networks

Small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated, but it does need to be taken seriously.
