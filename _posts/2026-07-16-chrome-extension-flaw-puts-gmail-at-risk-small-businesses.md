  # Chrome Extension Flaw Puts Your Business Gmail at Risk—Here's What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running your business from your phone and laptop—managing bookings, sending quotes, chasing invoices—there's a good chance your Gmail account is the heart of your operation. And right now, there's a security flaw that could hand the keys to that account straight to cybercriminals.

A vulnerability nicknamed "ClaudeBleed" has been discovered in a popular Chrome extension called Claude for Chrome. The flaw allows dodgy browser extensions to piggyback on Claude's permissions and quietly access your Gmail, Google Drive, and other connected accounts—without you knowing a thing.

## What Is Claude for Chrome?

Claude is an AI assistant made by a company called Anthropic. Many business owners have been using the Claude for Chrome extension to help draft emails, summarise documents, or get quick answers while they work online. It's designed to make life easier—but this security hole means it could also make your business data dangerously exposed.

## How the ClaudeBleed Flaw Works

Here's the problem: when you install Claude for Chrome, you grant it permission to read and interact with your emails and documents. That's necessary for it to do its job. But the ClaudeBleed vulnerability means other malicious extensions you've installed—even ones that look harmless—can exploit those same permissions.

In plain English: a dodgy extension pretending to be a colour picker, PDF converter, or coupon finder could be silently reading your client emails, pinching financial records, or even sending messages as you.

## Why This Matters for Small Business Cyber Security

You might think, "I only install extensions I trust." But here's the thing—many malicious extensions look legitimate. They rack up positive reviews (often fake), appear in the Chrome Web Store, and do what they promise… while also doing something else in the background.

For Sunshine Coast small business owners, the stakes are high:

- **A landscape gardener** could have client contact lists stolen and sold to competitors or scammers
- **An accountant** might unknowingly expose sensitive financial data for multiple clients
- **A café owner** using Gmail to manage supplier orders and staff rosters could have their account hijacked, leading to fraudulent orders or payroll tampering
- **A tradie** sending quotes via email could have banking details intercepted and changed by criminals

This isn't a far-fetched scenario. SME data breaches are increasing across Australia, and many start with something as simple as a browser extension with too much access.

## What You Should Do Right Now

The good news? You're not powerless. Here's how to protect your business:

### Review Your Chrome Extensions

- Open Chrome and type `chrome://extensions` into the address bar
- Look at every extension you have installed
- Remove anything you don't recognise or don't use regularly
- Be especially wary of extensions that request access to "read and change all your data on websites"

### Update Your Browser and Extensions

- Make sure Chrome itself is up to date
- Check that Claude for Chrome (if you use it) has been updated to the latest version
- Enable automatic updates wherever possible

### Consider a Professional IT Security Audit

Even if you don't have an IT team, it's worth getting a professional set of eyes on your setup. A proper cyber security Sunshine Coast audit can uncover risks you didn't know existed—and give you a clear action plan to fix them.

### Use Strong, Unique Passwords

- Don't reuse your Gmail password anywhere else
- Consider a password manager to keep track
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your Google account
