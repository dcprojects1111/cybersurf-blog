  # Russian Hackers Target Aussie Small Businesses with Password Spray Attacks

## What's happening with cyber security on the Sunshine Coast right now

If you run a small business here on the Sunshine Coast, you need to know about a cyber threat that's hitting Australian businesses hard right now. Russian-backed hackers are using something called "password spray attacks" to break into business accounts, and they're specifically targeting small operators like tradies, retailers, and hospitality businesses who don't have big IT security teams watching their backs.

Unlike the sophisticated hacking you see in movies, this method is surprisingly simple — and that's exactly why it works so well against small business owners.

## How password spray attacks actually work

Here's what these hackers are doing:

**The basic method:**

- They collect lists of real email addresses from businesses (often from your website, social media, or data breaches)
- Instead of trying thousands of passwords on one account (which triggers security alerts), they try one common password across thousands of accounts
- They use passwords like "Password123", "Summer2024", or "Welcome1" — simple passwords that plenty of people still use
- They work slowly and quietly, so security systems don't notice anything suspicious

Think about this scenario: You're an electrician on the Sunshine Coast, and you use the same simple password for your Xero account, your Gmail, and your supplier portal. A hacker only needs to guess that one password correctly, and suddenly they've got access to your invoicing system, your customer database, and your business banking details.

## Why this is a real problem for Sunshine Coast SMEs

**You might think: "Why would hackers target my small business?"**

The truth is, small business cyber security is often the weakest link. Hackers know that:

- Most sole traders and micro-businesses don't have IT support
- Many business owners use simple, easy-to-remember passwords
- Small businesses often use the same password across multiple platforms
- There's real money to be made — either by stealing directly or holding your data to ransom

An SME data breach doesn't just mean lost data. For a local café owner, it could mean losing access to your online ordering system during your busiest weekend. For an Airbnb host, it could mean hackers taking over your listing and redirecting booking payments to themselves. For a landscaper, it could mean losing years of customer records and job schedules.

## What you can do to protect your business

The good news? You don't need to become a tech expert or hire an expensive IT team. Here's what actually works:

**Immediate steps:**

- Use different passwords for different accounts — especially for banking, accounting software, and email
- Make your passwords longer and less obvious (think phrases rather than single words)
- Turn on two-factor authentication wherever it's offered (that's the extra code sent to your phone)
- Be suspicious of any unexpected "password reset" emails

**Smart business habits:**

- Never share passwords via text or email
- Update passwords if you've let a staff member go
- Check your bank and business accounts regularly for anything unusual
- Consider a password manager app to keep track of everything securely
