  # Apple's Hide My Email Feature Has a Serious Flaw — And It Could Affect Your Business

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast and using an iPhone or Mac to manage your accounts, bookings, or customer data, you need to know about a security problem that Apple still hasn't fixed — even after a year of knowing about it.

A cybersecurity researcher recently went public with a vulnerability in Apple's "Hide My Email" feature, and what he discovered should concern any sole trader or micro-business owner who relies on Apple devices to run their business.

## What Is Hide My Email?

Hide My Email is an Apple privacy feature that creates random email addresses to use when you sign up for websites or apps. The idea is simple: instead of giving out your real email address to every online service, Apple generates a unique email that forwards messages to your actual inbox.

It's a great privacy tool in theory — and many Sunshine Coast business owners use it without thinking twice. But there's a problem that's been sitting unfixed for 12 months.

## The Problem That Apple Won't Fix

The researcher found that Hide My Email doesn't actually hide your email as well as it should. Without getting too technical, the flaw means that under certain circumstances, your real email address can be exposed even when you're using the Hide My Email feature.

Think about what that means for your business. Perhaps you're:

- An electrician who uses Hide My Email when signing up for supplier portals
- A café owner managing multiple delivery service accounts
- An Airbnb host protecting your personal email from guest platforms
- A beauty salon owner registering for booking systems and product suppliers

You trusted Apple to keep your real email private. But that protection has cracks in it.

## Why This Matters for Small Business Cyber Security

Your email address is more than just a contact method — it's often the key to your entire digital business life. When criminals get hold of your real email address, they can:

- Target you with convincing phishing scams that mention your actual business
- Attempt password resets on your banking, invoicing, or booking systems
- Build a profile of which services you use to craft better attacks
- Sell your verified business email to other scammers

For a tradie juggling jobs from their phone or a retail shop owner managing online orders, an SME data breach often starts with something as simple as an exposed email address.

## What Makes This Worse

The most concerning part? Apple has known about this vulnerability for a full year. The researcher followed responsible disclosure practices — he told Apple privately, gave them time to fix it, and only went public when nothing happened.

This isn't about bashing Apple. Their products are generally secure. But even big tech companies sometimes drop the ball, and when they do, small business owners on the Sunshine Coast are left exposed.

## What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Should Do

You don't need to panic, but you should take this seriously:

- **Check what you've used Hide My Email for** — especially any business-critical accounts like Xero, Square, banking apps, or booking systems
- **Consider using your real business email for important accounts** — at least until Apple fixes this properly
- **Enable two-factor authentication** on every business account that offers it
- **Be extra vigilant about phishing emails** — if scammers have obtained your real address, expect more targeted attempts
