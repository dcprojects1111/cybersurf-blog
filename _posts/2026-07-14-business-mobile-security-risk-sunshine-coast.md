**Understanding the Device You Run Your Business From**

If you're like most Sunshine Coast small business owners, your smartphone isn't just a phone—it's your entire business system. Your Square or Tyro payment app, your client bookings, your Xero login, your customer WhatsApp groups, and probably your business banking too.

## Why Small Business Cyber Security Starts at Home

For micro-businesses and sole traders, there's rarely a clear line between personal and professional digital life. The same device that holds your family photos also stores:

- Your business banking apps
- Customer contact details and booking histories
- Login credentials to Canva, Google Business, and social media accounts
- Access to your payment processing systems
- Trade supplier account information
- Email conversations with clients and contractors

When we hand that device to our kids—or partner, or friend—we're essentially giving them the keys to our entire business operation.

## What Actually Works: Practical Steps for Sunshine Coast Business Owners

You don't need an expensive IT security audit or a degree in tech to protect yourself. Here's what actually makes a difference:

### Device Boundaries

- Keep one phone for business, one for personal use if possible (even an older model for the kids)
- If you can only have one device, create a separate user profile for family members
- Never share your unlock PIN or face ID with anyone—including family

### App Hygiene

- Only download apps from official stores (Google Play or Apple App Store)
- Check app permissions—does that game really need access to your contacts and camera?
- Regularly delete apps you're not using
- Enable two-factor authentication on every business app that offers it

### Password Protection

- Use different passwords for business and personal accounts
- Consider a password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password
- Never save passwords in your phone's notes app
- Change passwords immediately if someone else has used your device

### Regular Check-ins

- Review your business account activity weekly
- Check your sent emails folder for anything you didn't send
- Monitor your business bank accounts for unfamiliar transactions
- Look for apps you don't remember installing
