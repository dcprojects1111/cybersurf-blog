  # Has Your Business Login Been Compromised? Why Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Need to Check Now

## Free Trials End, But Security Threats Don't

You might have seen the news about AI companies extending access to their premium tools. While that's interesting for tech enthusiasts, there's a more pressing issue hiding in plain sight: the way we sign up for "free trials" and online business tools is creating serious cyber security risks for Sunshine Coast small businesses.

Think about how many online services you've signed up for in the past year. Your booking system. That accounting app your mate recommended. The rostering tool you tried for two weeks then forgot about. Each one required an email address and password – and each one is now a potential entry point for cybercriminals.

## The Real Threat to Your Business Logins

Here's what's happening: when companies offer free trials or change their subscription models, they often leave old accounts sitting dormant. You might have moved on to a different app, but your business email and password are still stored in their system.

If that company experiences a data breach – and thousands do every year – your login details can end up on the dark web. Criminals then use automated tools to test those credentials across hundreds of other services, from banking apps to government portals.

**This isn't a theoretical problem.** Last month, a landscaper in Maroochydore discovered someone had accessed his Xero account and changed payment details for several invoices. The money went to a scammer's account instead of his. The entry point? A password he'd reused from a free project management tool he'd tried years earlier.

## Why Small Business Cyber Security Gets Overlooked

If you're running your entire operation from your phone – managing bookings, sending invoices, ordering supplies – you're not thinking about IT security audits. You don't have time, and you probably don't have a dedicated IT person.

But that's exactly why you're a target.

Cybercriminals know that sole traders and micro-businesses:

- Often reuse the same password across multiple platforms
- Rarely enable two-factor authentication
- Don't monitor their old accounts for suspicious activity
- Can't afford expensive downtime or recovery costs

An SME data breach doesn't make headlines like a big corporate hack, but it can be devastating. When your QuickBooks gets locked, your client database disappears, or your Google Business Profile gets hijacked, you lose income immediately.

## What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Should Do

The good news is that basic cyber security for small business doesn't require technical expertise or a big budget.

**Start with these three steps:**

- **Audit your accounts:** Make a list of every online service you've ever signed up for. Include the ones you no longer use.
- **Check for breaches:** Find out if any of those services have leaked your information. This is simpler than it sounds.
- **Update your passwords:** Use unique passwords for important accounts – especially banking, accounting software, and anything connected to customer data.

For Sunshine Coast tradies, accommodation hosts, salon owners, and retail operators, the biggest vulnerability isn't sophisticated hacking – it's old passwords from forgotten accounts.
