  # Has Your Business Login Already Been Stolen? The Credential Stuffing Risk Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you're running your landscaping business, beauty salon, or Airbnb from your phone and laptop, there's a cyber security threat you've probably never heard of—but it might already be affecting you right now.

It's called credential stuffing, and it's one of the fastest-growing ways criminals are breaking into small business accounts across Australia. The good news? Once you understand how it works, protecting yourself is surprisingly straightforward.

## What Is Credential Stuffing?

Credential stuffing sounds technical, but the concept is simple. Cybercriminals get hold of millions of stolen usernames and passwords from old data breaches—think LinkedIn, Facebook, Adobe, or any of the hundreds of companies that have been hacked over the years. Then they use automated software to try those same login combinations on other websites and apps.

Why does this work? Because most of us reuse the same password across multiple accounts.

Let's say you signed up for a loyalty program at a big retail chain five years ago using your email and a password you also use for Xero, Square, or your booking system. If that retailer gets breached and your login details end up on the dark web, criminals can stuff those credentials into dozens of other platforms until something clicks.

And when it does, they're in.

## Why Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Are Prime Targets

You might think, "I'm just a gardener" or "I only run a small café—why would anyone target me?" The truth is, criminals aren't targeting you personally. They're targeting everyone, using bots that can test thousands of logins per second.

SME data breach incidents are rising precisely because small businesses often lack dedicated IT security audits or support. You're busy running your business, not monitoring login attempts or setting up two-factor authentication.

Here's a real-world scenario: Imagine you're an electrician on the Sunshine Coast. You use the same password for your Facebook account, your supplier portal, and your MYOB invoicing app. A data breach at an unrelated company exposes your Facebook login. Suddenly, a bot tries that same combination on MYOB—and gains access to your client list, invoices, banking details, and ABN.

Within hours, your clients are receiving fake invoices. Your reputation takes a hit. Your account is locked. And you're left scrambling to regain control.

## Warning Signs Your Accounts May Be Compromised

How do you know if credential stuffing has already hit your business? Watch for these red flags:

- Unexpected password reset emails you didn't request
- Notifications about logins from unfamiliar locations or devices
- Customers mentioning they received strange messages or invoices from you
- Being suddenly locked out of key business apps or social media
- Unusual activity in your bank accounts or payment platforms

Even if you haven't noticed these signs, your login details could still be circulating online, waiting to be used.

## Simple Steps to Protect Your Business

Small business cyber security doesn't have to be complicated. Here's what you can do today:

**Use unique passwords for every account.** A password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password makes this easy—it remembers them for you.

**Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)** wherever possible. Even if someone has your password, they can't get in without the second code sent to your phone.

**Check if your email has been compromised.** Free tools exist, but they only show part of the picture.

**Stay alert to phishing emails** that try to trick you into giving up your login details.
