  # Is Your Business App Access Safe? What Sunshine Coast Small Businesses Need to Know About Service Changes

## Why Service Disruptions Are a Cyber Security Issue

Most Sunshine Coast small business owners don't realize that relying entirely on a single app or service—without backup access to your own data—is a significant cyber security risk.

Here's what can go wrong:

**Loss of Access**
- Subscription changes might lock you out temporarily
- You could lose access to critical customer data
- Invoice histories and payment records might become unavailable

**Data Vulnerability**
- During transitions between services, your information is most exposed
- Hurried migrations often mean poor security choices
- You might accidentally share sensitive data with the wrong platform

**Business Continuity Risks**
- Without backups, a service change can halt your operations
- You might be forced to accept new terms that compromise your data security
- Emergency upgrades often bypass proper IT security audit processes

## Real-World Impact for Sunshine Coast Businesses

Let's make this concrete. An accountant on the Sunshine Coast using cloud-based practice management software suddenly faces a forced upgrade. The new pricing tier is triple the cost, or they can move to a "basic" plan that doesn't include access to three years of client tax records. They need those records—tax season is approaching.

In the rush to find an alternative, they might:
- Download all client data to a personal laptop (not encrypted)
- Email sensitive financial information to themselves
- Share access credentials with a family member to help sort things out
- Sign up for a new service without checking its security credentials

Each of these rushed decisions creates an SME data breach waiting to happen.

## What Small Business Cyber Security Actually Means

You don't need a tech degree to protect your business. Small business cyber security starts with three simple practices:

**Know Where Your Data Lives**
- List every app and service that holds customer information
- Confirm you can export your data at any time
- Keep local backups of critical business information

**Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket**
- Have a backup method for critical functions (invoicing, bookings, contacts)
- Keep copies of essential documents outside cloud services
- Know alternative providers before you need them

**Check Your Security Regularly**
- Review which services have access to your business accounts
- Change passwords when services change ownership or terms
- Watch for unusual activity in your accounts
