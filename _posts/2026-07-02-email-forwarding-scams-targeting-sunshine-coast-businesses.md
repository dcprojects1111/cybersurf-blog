  # Your Business Email Could Be Silently Forwarding to Criminals Right Now

**If you're running your Sunshine Coast business from your phone — managing bookings, sending invoices, or coordinating jobs via email — there's a threat you need to know about. And it's one that leaves no trace until it's too late.**

Cybercriminals are increasingly exploiting a simple email feature that most small business owners don't even know exists: auto-forwarding rules. These hidden rules can be planted in your inbox to silently send copies of every email you receive straight to a scammer's account. You'll never know it's happening.

## What Are Email Forwarding Rules?

Email forwarding rules are legitimate tools built into platforms like Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail. They're designed to help you manage your inbox — for example, automatically sending receipts to your accountant or forwarding supplier emails to your business partner.

But in the wrong hands, they become a weapon.

When a hacker gains access to your email account — often through a dodgy link, a weak password, or a data breach you didn't even know about — one of the first things they do is set up a hidden forwarding rule. From that moment on, every email you receive is quietly copied and sent to them.

## Why This Is Devastating for Small Businesses

Imagine you're a local electrician. A client emails you asking for a quote. You reply. They respond with payment details. But what you don't know is that a cybercriminal is reading every word of that conversation in real time.

They wait. Then, at just the right moment, they send a fake email from an account that looks almost identical to yours, telling the client your bank details have changed. The client pays the "new" account. The money disappears offshore. And you're left explaining to an angry customer why you never received their payment — and potentially facing legal trouble.

This exact scenario has played out hundreds of times across Australia. It's called Business Email Compromise (BEC), and it's one of the most damaging cyber threats facing SMEs today.

## It's Not Just About Money

For Sunshine Coast business owners, the damage goes beyond dollars. Consider:

- **An Airbnb host** whose inbox is forwarded to scammers — who now have access to guest details, booking confirmations, and even copies of IDs sent for verification
- **A hairdresser** who uses email to manage appointment reminders and client preferences — suddenly facing a privacy breach when sensitive customer information is exposed
- **A plumber** coordinating jobs with suppliers and subcontractors — now vulnerable to invoice fraud that could destroy professional relationships built over years

Your email account isn't just a communication tool. It's the nerve centre of your business. And if someone else is secretly reading it, they have everything they need to impersonate you, steal from your clients, or hold your data to ransom.

## How to Protect Your Business

The good news? Email forwarding rules are easy to spot and remove — once you know where to look. Here's what you can do today:

- **Check your forwarding settings** in Gmail, Outlook, or whatever platform you use (look under Settings > Forwarding)
- **Turn on two-factor authentication** — this stops hackers accessing your account even if they have your password
- **Use strong, unique passwords** for every business account
- **Be suspicious of urgent emails** asking you to click links or change payment details
