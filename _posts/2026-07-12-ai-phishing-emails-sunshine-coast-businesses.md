  # Cyber Criminals Are Using AI to Write More Convincing Scam Emails — Here's What Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Know

If you've noticed that scam emails are getting harder to spot lately, you're not imagining things. Cyber criminals are now using artificial intelligence to write phishing emails that sound completely legitimate — no more dodgy grammar or obvious spelling mistakes to tip you off.

For small business owners on the Sunshine Coast running everything from their phone, this is a game-changer. The old rules about spotting scams don't work as well anymore, and that's putting local tradies, hospitality operators, and service providers at serious risk.

## Why AI-Powered Scams Are Different

Traditional phishing emails were often easy to identify. They'd have weird phrasing, be addressed to "Dear Customer," or make claims that just didn't sound right. You could usually trust your gut.

Now, criminals are using the same AI tools that businesses use for customer service to craft emails that:

* Sound exactly like your bank, supplier, or software provider
* Address you by name and reference real transactions
* Use perfect Australian English with no spelling errors
* Create a sense of urgency without obvious scare tactics
* Match the writing style of legitimate companies perfectly

The technology can even research your business online and personalize the scam based on what you do and who you work with.

## Real Risks for Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

Imagine you're a plumber who's just finished a big job. You get an email that looks exactly like it's from Xero or MYOB, asking you to verify your account details because of "unusual activity." The email sounds professional, uses your name, and even references your recent invoicing. You click the link, enter your password, and suddenly someone overseas has access to all your financial records and client information.

Or picture this: you run a short-stay accommodation business with several properties listed online. You receive what appears to be a legitimate email from your booking platform about a policy update. You follow the instructions, and within hours, scammers have taken over your listing, changed the banking details, and are collecting payments from your guests while you're locked out of your own account.

These aren't far-fetched scenarios. They're happening right now to businesses just like yours, and the criminals behind them are using AI to make their attacks more convincing than ever.

## How to Protect Your Business

The good news is that cyber security for small businesses doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. Here's what you can do starting today:

### Check Before You Click

* Never click links in emails asking you to log in or verify details
* Instead, open your browser and go directly to the website
* If an email creates urgency, that's your signal to slow down, not speed up

### Enable Two-Factor Authentication

* Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) for every business account you use
* This means even if someone gets your password, they still can't access your accounts
* Most banking, accounting, and booking platforms offer this for free

### Verify Unusual Requests

* If you get an email asking for money, password resets, or account changes, call the company directly
* Use the phone number from their official website, not from the email

### Keep Your Devices Updated

* Enable automatic updates on your phone and computer
* These updates fix security holes that criminals exploit
