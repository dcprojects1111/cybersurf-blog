  # AI Agents Are Creating a Hidden Cybersecurity Risk for Sunshine Coast Small Businesses

If you run a small business on the Sunshine Coast, chances are you're already using artificial intelligence without even realising it. That chatbot on your website? AI. The smart scheduling tool that books your appointments? AI-powered. The automated invoice reminders from your accounting software? You guessed it — AI.

But here's what most sole traders and micro-business owners don't know: every one of these AI tools creates what cybersecurity experts call a "non-human identity" in your digital systems. And right now, these silent helpers are opening doors for cybercriminals that most small businesses don't even know exist.

## What Are Non-Human Identities?

Think of your business's digital world like your physical shop or worksite. You know exactly who has keys — you, maybe your partner, perhaps a trusted employee. But in your digital business, there are dozens of "keys" you've handed out without realising it:

- Apps that connect to your bank account to track expenses
- Booking systems that access your calendar
- Social media scheduling tools linked to your business pages
- Cloud storage services that sync across devices
- Payment processors connected to your online store

Each of these connections creates a digital identity that can log in and access your business information — even when you're not actively using them.

## Why This Matters for Your Sunshine Coast Business

Let me give you a real example. Imagine you're a landscaper in Buderim who uses a quoting app connected to your customer database. That app needs permission to read your client list, their addresses, and their contact details. It's incredibly convenient — until that app gets hacked or the company behind it suffers an SME data breach.

Suddenly, cybercriminals have a back door into your customer information. They didn't need to crack your password or trick you with a phishing email. They just walked through a door that was already open.

The problem is getting worse because AI tools are multiplying faster than ever. Every time you sign up for a new service with "Connect with Google" or "Link to Facebook," you're creating another non-human identity with access to your business data.

## The Identity Security Gap

Here's the uncomfortable truth: most small business owners on the Sunshine Coast have absolutely no idea how many of these digital identities exist in their business systems.

You might remember setting up your Square payment system two years ago, but do you remember that inventory app you tried for three weeks and then forgot about? It's probably still connected. Still has access. Still a potential entry point for cybercriminals looking for small business cyber security vulnerabilities to exploit.

This is what IT security professionals call the "identity security gap" — the growing disconnect between the digital access you've granted and your awareness of what's actually out there.

## What You Can Do About It

The good news? You don't need to become a tech expert to protect your business. Here's what matters:

**Start with visibility:** You need to know what's connected to what. Which apps can access your business banking? Which tools have permission to read your customer emails? Which services are linked to your social media accounts?

**Regular reviews:** Set a reminder every three months to review your connected apps and services. If you're not using it anymore, disconnect it.

**Understand your digital footprint:** Even simple steps toward understanding your cyber security sunshine coast exposure can dramatically reduce your risk.
