  # Job Scam Phishing Attack: Why Sunshine Coast Business Owners Need to Pay Attention

If you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, chances are you wear a lot of hats. Marketing might be one of them—even if it's just posting on Instagram or replying to Google reviews. But a sophisticated new scam doing the rounds should have every business owner on alert, especially if you or your staff ever look at job ads or respond to recruitment messages.

## What's Happening?

Cybercriminals are impersonating major brands like Adobe, Netflix, Coca-Cola, and OpenAI to run fake job interviews. The goal? To steal Google account passwords from people who manage marketing and social media.

Here's how it works: you receive a message—often via LinkedIn or email—offering an exciting job opportunity with a household name. The "recruiter" seems professional, the company logo looks right, and before you know it, you're clicking a link to "complete your application" or "schedule your interview."

That link takes you to a fake Google login page. You type in your email and password, thinking you're logging into a legitimate platform. Instead, you've just handed over the keys to your entire Google account—and possibly your business.

## Why Should Sunshine Coast Small Business Owners Care?

You might be thinking, "I'm not looking for a job—I *have* a job. I run my own business." Fair enough. But this phishing scam isn't just targeting job seekers. It's also hitting:

- Business owners who occasionally explore new opportunities or partnerships
- Staff members who manage your social media or bookings
- Anyone who uses Gmail, Google Drive, or Google Calendar for work

Imagine this: You run a café in Mooloolaba. Your casual manager gets one of these fake interview emails. She clicks the link, enters her Google password—and suddenly, the scammers have access to your supplier orders, staff rosters, customer email lists, and your business bank account recovery email.

Or picture this: You're an electrician using Google to manage your invoicing, job bookings, and client details. One dodgy click, and a scammer could lock you out of everything, change your passwords, and hold your business to ransom.

## The Local Cyber Security Reality

Small business cyber security on the Sunshine Coast isn't about having a dedicated IT team. Most sole traders and micro-businesses run everything from their phone. That makes phishing attacks like this especially dangerous—because one compromised Google account can unravel your whole operation.

An SME data breach doesn't always look like a Hollywood hack. Sometimes it's as simple as losing access to your bookings, your invoices, or your customer database because someone stole your login.

## How to Protect Your Business

You don't need to be a tech expert to stay safe. Here are a few simple habits that make a huge difference:

- **Be suspicious of unsolicited job offers**, especially if they come out of the blue or seem too good to be true
- **Check the URL** before entering your password—fake login pages often have slightly wrong web addresses
- **Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)** for your Google account. Even if someone gets your password, they can't get in without the second code
- **Don't reuse passwords** across different apps or accounts
- **Talk to your staff** if you have any—make sure they know what to watch for

## What If It's Already Too Late?

The scariest part? You might not realise your account has been compromised until it's too late. Scammers can sit quietly in your inbox, watching and waiting, learning about your business and your clients before they strike.

That's where a simple IT security audit can help. Even a basic check can tell you whether your email or business accounts have been part of a known data breach—and what you need to do about it.
