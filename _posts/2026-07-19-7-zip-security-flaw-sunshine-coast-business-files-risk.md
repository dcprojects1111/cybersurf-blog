  # Critical Security Update: Why Your Business Files Might Not Be as Safe as You Think

If you've ever downloaded a ZIP file to access a quote, contract, or customer photo gallery, you need to read this. A serious security flaw has just been discovered in 7-Zip, one of the world's most popular file compression programs — and it could let cybercriminals take control of your computer simply by getting you to open what looks like a harmless file.

For Sunshine Coast small business owners running operations from your phone or laptop, this isn't just tech news. It's a wake-up call about how easily everyday business tasks can become security nightmares.

## What Actually Happened?

7-Zip is free software that millions of people use to open compressed files — those .zip, .rar, and .7z files that arrive in your inbox or get downloaded from websites. The developers recently discovered a critical vulnerability that allows hackers to hide malicious code inside what appears to be a legitimate compressed file.

Here's the scary part: you don't need to run a suspicious program or click multiple warnings. Simply opening the wrong archive file could give attackers access to your system.

## Why Sunshine Coast SMEs Should Care

Think about your typical week. You might receive:

* Building plans or council documents from suppliers
* Photo galleries from contractors or clients
* Updated price lists from wholesalers
* Contract templates from your accountant
* Before-and-after photos for your portfolio

Any of these could arrive as a compressed file. If you're running your café's books, managing your electrical business invoicing, or coordinating your landscaping crew all from the same device, a single compromised file could expose everything.

Imagine this: You're managing a portfolio of three Airbnb properties on the Coast. A "guest" emails you compressed photos of alleged property damage. You open the file to review their claim. Within minutes, hackers have access to your booking system passwords, guest data, and banking details. Your listings get hijacked, your calendar wiped, and your income stream vanishes while you scramble to prove your identity to platform support.

That's the real-world impact of what seems like distant tech news.

## This Is Part of a Bigger Pattern

Small business cyber security isn't just about fancy corporate firewalls. An SME data breach often starts with something mundane — a compressed file, a fake invoice, a too-good-to-be-true supplier email.

Sole traders and micro-businesses on the Sunshine Coast are increasingly targeted because criminals know you're:

* Time-poor and less likely to scrutinise every email
* Handling everything yourself without IT support
* Using the same device for business and personal tasks
* Unlikely to have updated security software

The assumption that "I'm too small to be targeted" is exactly what makes you vulnerable.

## What You Should Do Right Now

**Update your software immediately.** If you use 7-Zip, update to version 26.02 or later. Even if you're not sure whether you use it, check your computer's installed programs.

**Be suspicious of compressed files from unknown senders.** If you weren't expecting it, don't open it — even if it looks legitimate.

**Consider a professional check.** Cyber security for Sunshine Coast businesses doesn't have to mean expensive consultants. Sometimes you just need to know where you stand.
