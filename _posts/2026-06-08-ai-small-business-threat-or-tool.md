# AI: Threat, Tool, or Both? What Small Business Owners Need to Know

You've probably noticed AI popping up everywhere lately — from chatbots answering customer queries to tools that write your social media posts. At the same time, you're hearing warnings about AI being used by scammers and hackers. So which is it? Is artificial intelligence something you should be using in your business, or something you should be worried about?

The honest answer: it's both. And if you're running a small business on the Sunshine Coast, understanding this double-edged reality matters more than you might think.

## Why People Are Concerned About AI

Public anxiety about AI isn't just media hype. There are legitimate reasons why the technology is making people nervous, particularly when it comes to security.

The main issue is this: the same AI tools that help you work faster and smarter are also helping criminals work faster and smarter. Think of it like power tools — a nail gun is brilliant when you're building a deck, but dangerous in the wrong hands. AI is similar, except it doesn't require any special equipment or training to misuse.

Here's what's changed in the threat landscape:

**Better phishing emails:** Those dodgy scam emails used to be easy to spot because of spelling mistakes and awkward phrasing. AI has changed that. Criminals now use it to write convincing, professional-looking messages that are much harder to identify as fraudulent.

**Deepfake voice calls:** We're now seeing cases where AI mimics someone's voice — imagine getting a call that sounds exactly like your accountant or bank manager, asking you to authorise a payment. It's not science fiction anymore.

**Automated attacks at scale:** Previously, scammers had to manually target victims. AI lets them personalise and launch thousands of attacks simultaneously, increasing the chances that someone in your team clicks the wrong link.

For a small Queensland business without a dedicated IT team, this escalation means the old approach of "just be careful" isn't quite enough anymore.

## But AI Is Also Part of the Solution

Here's the thing that often gets lost in the worry: cybersecurity tools are using the exact same technology to protect you.

Modern security software increasingly uses AI to spot unusual patterns — like a login attempt from an unfamiliar location, or someone trying to access files they don't normally use. It works a bit like a good guard dog that learns what's normal around your property and barks when something's off.

Anti-virus and email filtering systems now use AI to identify threats that haven't even been seen before, rather than just blocking known viruses. For small businesses, this means better protection without needing to understand how it all works under the hood.

### What This Means for Your Business

You don't need to become an AI expert, but you do need to adjust your thinking slightly. Here are two practical steps you can take this week:

- **Review your email security:** Make sure your business email has proper filtering turned on. If you're using basic Gmail or Outlook without business-grade protection, it's time for an upgrade. This is especially important if you handle customer data or process payments.

- **Brief your team on AI-powered scams:** Spend ten minutes in your next team meeting explaining that professional-looking emails and convincing phone calls can still be fake. The new rule: if something involves money or sensitive information, verify through a second channel — call the person back on a number you already have, don't use contact details from the email.

The cyber security landscape around the Sunshine Coast is the same as everywhere else: threats are getting more sophisticated, but so are the defences. The key is staying just informed enough to make sensible decisions.
