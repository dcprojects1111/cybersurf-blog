Even the most trusted tech companies are embedding hidden tracking into their systems—and it's happening without most users realising it.

## What Actually Happened?

Anthropic, the company behind the popular AI assistant Claude, recently admitted to hiding invisible tracking markers in code their AI creates for developers. They called it an "experiment," but it's sparked serious concerns about transparency and trust in the tech tools small businesses increasingly rely on.

The tracking was done using something called "prompt steganography"—essentially hiding secret messages inside normal-looking text that humans can't see, but computers can read.

While this specific case involved developers and coding, it highlights a much bigger issue: **the software and online tools you trust every day might be doing things behind the scenes you never agreed to.**
